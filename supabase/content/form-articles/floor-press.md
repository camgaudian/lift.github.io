# Floor Press

## Setup
- Lie on the floor with knees bent and feet flat, or legs extended for more stability practice.
- Hold a barbell or dumbbells at chest level; shoulder blades pinched down and back.
- Elbows rest on the floor at roughly 45–75° from the torso at the bottom position.
- If using a barbell, use a rack or spotter to hand off safely from the floor setup.

## Execution
- Press the weight up until elbows lock out comfortably; forearms vertical at the bottom stop.
- Lower with control until the upper arms contact the floor; pause briefly without bouncing.
- Press up and slightly back if using a barbell; keep wrists stacked over elbows.
- Maintain head, upper back, and hip contact with the floor throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Bouncing the elbows off the floor — reduces control and can stress the shoulders.
- Flaring elbows to 90° — increases shoulder strain compared with a moderate tuck.
- Over-arching the lower back — less common on the floor but still possible; keep ribs down.
- Cutting range short above the floor — the floor defines bottom position; use it consistently.

## Safety notes
- The floor limits shoulder extension at the bottom, which can help some lifters with shoulder discomfort.
- Stop for sharp shoulder, chest, or wrist pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if pressing variations aggravate prior shoulder injuries.
