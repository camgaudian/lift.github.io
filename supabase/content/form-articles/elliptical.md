# Elliptical

## Setup
- Step onto the pedals once the machine is on; grasp the handles lightly for balance, not to bear weight.
- Stand tall with a slight knee bend; center your weight over the mid-foot.
- Set resistance and incline to match your fitness level and session goal.
- Begin with a smooth, low-resistance warm-up before increasing intensity.

## Execution
- Drive the pedals in a smooth elliptical path; keep heels in contact or nearly in contact throughout.
- Engage the core and maintain upright posture; avoid leaning heavily on the handles.
- Use a natural arm swing with the moving handles or keep arms fixed for more leg emphasis.
- Cool down gradually; reduce resistance and pace before stepping off.

## Common mistakes
- Locking the knees at the top of each stride — increases joint stress.
- Slouching forward and dumping weight into the handles — reduces calorie burn and core work.
- Starting too fast or too steep — leads to poor form and early fatigue.
- Holding breath or shallow breathing at higher intensities — breathe rhythmically.

## Safety notes
- Face forward and wait for the pedals to stop before dismounting.
- Stop if you feel dizziness, chest pain, or unusual joint discomfort.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician before high-intensity cardio if you have cardiovascular or joint conditions.
