# Sumo Deadlift

## Setup
- Stand with feet wider than shoulder width; toes pointed out 30–45°.
- Grip the bar inside the knees — double overhand or mixed grip as appropriate.
- Drop hips so shins are near vertical; chest up and shoulders over or slightly in front of the bar.
- Brace the core; pull slack from the bar before initiating the lift.

## Execution
- Drive through the floor; push knees out to track over the toes.
- Keep the bar close to the body as it rises past the knees.
- Lock out at the top with hips and knees extended; do not hyperextend the lower back.
- Lower by pushing hips back first; control the bar to the floor.

## Common mistakes
- Hips shooting up before the bar moves — turns the pull into a stiff-leg deadlift.
- Knees caving inward — reduces power and stresses the knee joint.
- Bar drifting forward — increases lower-back load.
- Rounding the upper back at setup — raises injury risk under heavy load.

## Safety notes
- Use bumper plates and a flat platform when dropping reps from height.
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back, hip, or groin pain.
- Consult a qualified coach for form checks before adding significant load.
