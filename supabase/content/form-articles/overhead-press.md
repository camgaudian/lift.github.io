# Overhead Press

## Setup
- Stand with feet hip-width; bar at collarbone height in a rack or clean position.
- Grip slightly wider than shoulders; elbows slightly in front of the bar.
- Brace the core and glutes; squeeze the quads to avoid excessive back arch.

## Execution
- Press the bar straight up, moving the head back slightly so the bar clears the face.
- Lock out with the bar over mid-foot, shoulders, and hips aligned.
- Bring the head forward through the arms at the top; full elbow extension.
- Lower with control to the starting position at the upper chest/clavicle.

## Common mistakes
- Excessive lower-back arch — reduce weight and tighten glutes/core.
- Pressing around the face instead of moving the head back — bar path goes forward.
- Flared ribs at lockout — loss of core brace.
- Partial range — bar should reach full lockout overhead.

## Safety notes
- Avoid behind-the-neck pressing if you lack shoulder mobility.
- Stop for sharp shoulder, neck, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if overhead mobility is limited.
