# Cable Face Pull

## Setup
- Attach a rope to a high pulley; grip each end with thumbs toward you (overhand-neutral hybrid).
- Step back until arms are extended; set feet in a staggered or hip-width stance.
- Pull shoulder blades down and back before you start.
- Choose a moderate weight — this is a rear-delt and external-rotation exercise, not a heavy row.

## Execution
- Pull the rope toward your face, splitting the ends apart as your hands pass your ears.
- Lead with elbows high and wide; externally rotate so knuckles face back at the finish.
- Pause briefly with rear delts and mid-back engaged.
- Return with control without letting shoulders round forward at the end.

## Common mistakes
- Using too much weight — turns the movement into a horizontal row with poor shoulder rotation.
- Elbows dropping below shoulder height — reduces rear-delt emphasis.
- Shrugging traps up toward ears instead of retracting the scapulae.
- Rocking the torso excessively — keep the core braced and movement controlled.

## Safety notes
- Prioritize shoulder-friendly load and full external rotation over heavy stacks.
- Stop if you feel pinching in the front of the shoulder.
- Consult a coach if you use face pulls to address shoulder pain — form and programming matter.
