# Front Raise

## Setup
- Stand tall with feet hip-width; hold dumbbells in front of the thighs, palms toward the body.
- Brace the core lightly; keep shoulders down before starting.
- Elbows stay nearly straight with a soft bend; wrists neutral.
- Choose a weight that allows strict reps without torso swing.

## Execution
- Raise the arms forward to shoulder height or slightly below; lead with the elbows, not the hands.
- Pause briefly at the top without shrugging the shoulders toward the ears.
- Lower with control to the thighs; maintain tension through the negative.
- Exhale on the way up if helpful; keep the torso still throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Swinging the hips and leaning back — reduces deltoid work and stresses the lower back.
- Lifting above shoulder height — increases impingement risk for many lifters.
- Shrugging at the top — shifts work to the traps instead of the anterior delts.
- Using momentum from alternating arms too quickly — sacrifices control.

## Safety notes
- Light to moderate loads are appropriate; front raises are an isolation movement.
- Stop for sharp anterior shoulder pain, especially when raising above 90°.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if overhead or front-raise patterns irritate the shoulders.
