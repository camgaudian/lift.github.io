# Hip Thrust

## Setup
- Upper back on a bench at shoulder-blade level; feet flat, knees bent about 90° at the top.
- Bar pad over the hips; shoulder blades pinned to the bench.
- Chin slightly tucked; ribs down before each rep.

## Execution
- Drive through the heels; extend hips to full lockout.
- Squeeze glutes at the top; shins vertical or slightly angled back.
- Lower with control until hips are below full extension without losing bench contact.
- Keep the rib cage down; avoid hyperextending the lower back at the top.

## Common mistakes
- Pushing through toes — reduces glute emphasis.
- Hyperextending the lumbar spine — finish with glutes, not lower back.
- Feet too far forward or back — adjust so shins are roughly vertical at lockout.
- Short range — hips should reach full extension.

## Safety notes
- Secure the bench so it does not slide.
- Stop for sharp hip or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if you feel the movement mainly in the quads or lower back.
