# Chest-Supported Row

## Setup
- Set an incline bench to roughly 30–45 degrees or use a dedicated chest-supported row machine.
- Lie chest-down with feet planted; grip the handles or bar with a neutral or overhand grip.
- Retract shoulder blades slightly before lifting; brace your core against the pad.
- Choose a load that allows full range without lifting your chest off the pad.

## Execution
- Pull elbows back toward your hips or rib cage, squeezing mid-back at the top.
- Keep chest glued to the pad — no jerking the torso to start the rep.
- Lower under control until arms are extended without protracting shoulders excessively.
- Maintain a neutral neck; look at the floor slightly ahead of the bench.

## Common mistakes
- Lifting chest off the pad to heave weight — removes the point of chest support.
- Shrugging traps instead of retracting and depressing the scapulae.
- Pulling too high toward the shoulders — reduces lat engagement.
- Using momentum from a hip drive when feet are poorly planted.

## Safety notes
- Adjust pad height so shoulders are not impinged at the bottom stretch.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder or lower-back pain.
- See a coach to match grip width and elbow path to your shoulder mobility.
