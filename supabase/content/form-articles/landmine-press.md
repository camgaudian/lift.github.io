# Landmine Press

## Setup
- Anchor one end of a barbell in a landmine base or corner; load the free end as needed.
- Stand facing the bar or in a half-kneeling or split stance for stability.
- Hold the end of the bar at chest height with one or both hands; core braced and ribs down.
- Set shoulders down and back before pressing.

## Execution
- Press the bar up and forward along the landmine arc until arms are extended without locking aggressively.
- Keep the torso stable; avoid excessive leaning or rotating unless performing a deliberate rotational variation.
- Lower with control back to the start position at the upper chest or shoulder.
- Exhale on the press; maintain a neutral wrist aligned with the forearm.

## Common mistakes
- Overarching the lower back — often from pressing too heavy or poor core bracing.
- Shrugging the shoulders at lockout — reduces shoulder stability.
- Pressing straight up instead of following the landmine path — fights the bar angle.
- Flaring the elbow too wide in single-arm variations — can irritate the shoulder.

## Safety notes
- Ensure the bar is securely anchored before loading.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder, elbow, or lower-back pain.
- Start light when learning half-kneeling or rotational variations.
