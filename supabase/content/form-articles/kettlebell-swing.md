# Kettlebell Swing

## Setup
- Place the kettlebell slightly in front of you; hinge at the hips and grip the handle with both hands.
- Stand with feet shoulder-width or slightly wider; toes turned out slightly.
- Set a flat back, braced core, and shoulders packed down.
- Hike the bell back between your legs to start momentum — do not squat the weight up.

## Execution
- Drive the hips forward explosively to swing the bell to chest height (Russian swing) or overhead if trained for it.
- Arms stay relaxed; the bell floats from hip power, not a front raise.
- Allow the bell to swing back between the legs as you hinge; knees stay soft but movement is hip-dominant.
- Exhale at the top of each swing; reset posture if the lower back rounds.

## Common mistakes
- Squatting instead of hinging — turns the swing into a front raise with poor mechanics.
- Using the arms to lift the bell — fatigues the shoulders and limits power.
- Overextending the lower back at the top — hyperextends the spine instead of locking out with glutes.
- Letting the bell drift too far forward on the backswing — pulls you off balance.

## Safety notes
- Master the hip hinge with a light bell before adding load or height.
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back or hamstring pain.
- Keep clear space in front and behind you; the bell travels fast on the backswing.
