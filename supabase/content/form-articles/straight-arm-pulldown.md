# Straight-Arm Pulldown

## Setup
- Attach a straight bar or rope to a high cable pulley.
- Stand facing the stack with feet shoulder-width; slight knee bend for stability.
- Grip the bar overhand at shoulder width; arms extended with a soft elbow lock.
- Hinge slightly at the hips; brace the core and keep the chest up.

## Execution
- Pull the bar down in an arc toward the thighs using the lats, not the arms.
- Keep elbows straight but not hyperextended throughout the movement.
- Squeeze the lats at the bottom; control the return to the starting position.
- Exhale on the pull; maintain a stable torso without swinging.

## Common mistakes
- Bending the elbows — turns the movement into a triceps pressdown.
- Using body momentum and leaning back — reduces lat isolation.
- Shrugging shoulders toward the ears — shifts tension to the traps.
- Pulling behind the body — can stress the shoulders unnecessarily.

## Safety notes
- Use a weight you can control through the full range without jerking.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder, elbow, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if you have shoulder impingement or prior rotator cuff issues.
