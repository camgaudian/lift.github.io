# Decline Sit-Up

## Setup
- Secure ankles under the decline bench pads; lie back with knees bent and feet anchored.
- Place hands lightly behind the head or across the chest; avoid pulling on the neck.
- Brace the core before initiating the first rep.
- Choose a decline angle appropriate to your experience and spinal tolerance.

## Execution
- Curl the torso up by flexing the spine; lead with the chest, not the chin.
- Rise until the upper back clears the bench or you reach a controlled peak contraction.
- Lower slowly to the start without collapsing; maintain tension through the abs.
- Exhale on the way up; inhale on the descent.

## Common mistakes
- Yanking the head forward with the hands — strains the neck and reduces ab work.
- Using hip flexor momentum to swing up — keep the movement trunk-focused.
- Dropping uncontrolled on the negative — increases spinal stress.
- Setting the bench too steep too soon — compromises form and comfort.

## Safety notes
- Reduce decline angle or switch to a crunch variation if you feel lower-back discomfort.
- Avoid maximal spinal flexion if you have disc-related symptoms unless cleared by a clinician.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if sit-ups consistently provoke neck or back pain.
