# Deadlift

## Setup
- Stand with mid-foot under the bar; feet hip-width; shins close to the bar.
- Hinge and grip the bar just outside the legs; shoulders slightly in front of the bar.
- Flatten the back; brace the core; pull slack out of the bar before lifting.

## Execution
- Drive through the floor; extend knees and hips together.
- Keep the bar close to the body; drag it up the shins and thighs.
- Stand tall at lockout with hips and knees fully extended; do not hyperextend the lower back.
- Lower by hinging hips back first, then bending knees once the bar passes them.

## Common mistakes
- Rounding the lower back — high injury risk; reduce weight and reset form.
- Bar drifting forward — increases strain on the lower back.
- Jerking the bar off the floor — breaks tension and disrupts the pull.
- Hitting the knees on the way down — path is too vertical; hinge first.

## Safety notes
- Avoid maximal singles without experience; build gradually.
- Stop immediately for sharp lower-back or hamstring pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if you have a history of back issues.
