# T-Bar Row

## Setup
- Load one end of a barbell into a landmine corner or T-bar station.
- Straddle the bar with feet shoulder-width; hinge at the hips to roughly 45°.
- Grip the handle with both hands; brace the core and keep the back flat.
- Let the arms hang straight; shoulders down and back before pulling.

## Execution
- Pull the handle toward the lower chest or upper abdomen; elbows drive back.
- Squeeze the shoulder blades together at the top of the rep.
- Lower with control until arms are extended; maintain the hip hinge throughout.
- Exhale on the pull; avoid jerking the weight with the lower back.

## Common mistakes
- Standing too upright — reduces lat engagement and invites lower-back rounding.
- Using momentum and heaving the torso — shifts load off the back muscles.
- Shrugging at the top — overuses the traps instead of the mid-back.
- Partial range of motion — missing the stretch at the bottom.

## Safety notes
- Secure the barbell in the landmine base before loading plates.
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back, shoulder, or biceps pain.
- Consult a qualified coach for form checks before adding significant load.
