# V-Up

## Setup
- Lie flat on a mat with arms extended overhead and legs straight.
- Press the lower back gently toward the floor; engage the core before moving.
- Point the toes and keep legs together for the duration of the set.
- Choose a range you can control without using momentum.

## Execution
- Simultaneously lift the legs and torso; reach hands toward the toes at the top.
- Form a V shape at the peak; pause briefly with control.
- Lower arms and legs with control back to the starting position.
- Exhale on the lift; avoid pulling the neck forward with the hands.

## Common mistakes
- Using momentum and rocking — reduces core engagement.
- Pulling on the neck to sit up — strains the cervical spine.
- Bending knees excessively — makes the movement easier but less effective.
- Dropping legs and torso uncontrolled — increases lower-back stress.

## Safety notes
- Modify by bending knees or performing dead bugs if full V-ups are too demanding.
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back or hip flexor pain.
- Consult a qualified clinician if you have diastasis recti or recent abdominal surgery.
