# Assault Bike

## Setup
- Adjust the seat so your knee is slightly bent at the bottom of the pedal stroke.
- Grip the moving handles lightly; set feet securely on the pedals.
- Sit tall with a neutral spine; brace lightly before starting.
- Choose a resistance level appropriate for your interval or steady-state goal.

## Execution
- Drive with legs first, then add arm pull on the handles for full-body power.
- Push and pull smoothly — avoid jerky, asymmetric strokes.
- Maintain steady breathing; match effort to your target heart rate or interval plan.
- Keep shoulders relaxed; let the legs do most of the work on lower-body-focused sessions.

## Common mistakes
- Seat too low — increases knee stress and reduces power.
- Hunching over the handles — restricts breathing and rounds the upper back.
- Gripping handles too tightly — wastes energy and tenses the upper body.
- Starting intervals too hard — leads to early burnout and poor form.

## Safety notes
- Warm up at easy resistance before high-intensity intervals.
- Stop if you feel chest pain, dizziness, or unusual shortness of breath.
- See a coach or clinician before intense conditioning if you have cardiovascular concerns.
