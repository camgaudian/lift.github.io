# Reverse Pec Deck

## Setup
- Adjust the seat so handles or pads align with shoulder height when seated facing the pad.
- Sit chest against the pad; feet flat on the floor, core lightly braced.
- Grip the handles or place forearms on the pads with arms extended in front.

## Execution
- Pull the arms back in a wide arc, squeezing the rear delts and upper back.
- Keep the chest in contact with the pad; avoid shrugging the traps excessively.
- Return with control to the starting position; maintain a slight bend in the elbows.
- Focus on moving through the shoulders, not the lower back.

## Common mistakes
- Using too much weight and jerking — prioritize control and rear-delt engagement.
- Lifting the chest off the pad — reduce weight and stay pinned to the seat.
- Shrugging at the end range — keep shoulders down and back.
- Hyperextending the lower back — movement should come from the shoulders only.

## Safety notes
- Start light; rear delts respond to moderate load and controlled reps.
- Stop for sharp shoulder or upper-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician for prior shoulder impingement issues.
