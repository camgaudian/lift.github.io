# Push Press

## Setup
- Stand with feet hip-width; bar at shoulder height in a rack or front-rack position.
- Grip slightly wider than shoulders; elbows slightly in front of the bar.
- Brace the core and glutes; dip by bending knees and hips slightly, torso upright.

## Execution
- Drive explosively through the legs to push the bar overhead; extend hips and knees.
- As the bar passes the face, press with the arms to full lockout overhead.
- Lock out with the bar over mid-foot, shoulders, and hips aligned.
- Lower the bar to the shoulders with control and reset for the next rep.

## Common mistakes
- Turning the dip into a squat — keep the dip shallow and vertical.
- Pressing before the legs finish driving — timing the leg drive and arm press is key.
- Excessive back lean — stay braced; reduce weight if the torso collapses backward.
- Pressing around the face — move the head back slightly so the bar travels straight up.

## Safety notes
- Clear overhead space and use a rack with safeties when training heavy.
- Stop for sharp shoulder, wrist, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach before heavy push pressing with prior shoulder injuries.
