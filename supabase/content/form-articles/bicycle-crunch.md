# Bicycle Crunch

## Setup
- Lie on your back with hands lightly behind your head — do not pull on your neck.
- Lift shoulder blades off the floor and bring knees to roughly 90 degrees.
- Brace your core; press your lower back gently toward the floor.
- Start with slow, controlled reps before adding speed.

## Execution
- Rotate your torso to bring one elbow toward the opposite knee while extending the other leg.
- Switch sides in a pedaling motion; focus on rib cage rotation, not just elbow movement.
- Exhale as you crunch; keep the extended leg low enough to maintain core tension.
- Move deliberately — quality rotation beats fast, sloppy reps.

## Common mistakes
- Yanking the head forward with your hands — strains the neck.
- Rotating only the elbows without turning the thoracic spine.
- Letting the lower back arch off the floor as the legs extend.
- Racing through reps with minimal core engagement.

## Safety notes
- Reduce range or speed if you feel neck strain.
- Stop if you experience sharp lower-back or hip flexor pain.
- See a coach if core exercises consistently aggravate your neck or lower back.
