# Reverse Lunge

## Setup
- Stand tall with feet hip-width; hands on hips, at sides, or holding dumbbells.
- Brace the core; gaze forward with chest up and shoulders relaxed.
- Shift weight to the working leg before stepping back.

## Execution
- Step one foot straight back; lower until both knees bend to roughly 90°.
- Front knee stays over the mid-foot; back knee approaches but does not slam the floor.
- Drive through the front heel to return to standing; alternate legs or complete one side first.
- Keep the torso upright; avoid leaning forward excessively.

## Common mistakes
- Front knee collapsing inward — push the knee out in line with the toes.
- Short step back — take a long enough step so the front shin stays relatively vertical.
- Torso pitching forward — brace the core and keep the chest up.
- Pushing off the back foot only — drive primarily through the front leg.

## Safety notes
- Use support or reduce range if balance is limited.
- Stop for sharp knee, hip, or ankle pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician for prior knee or hip injuries.
