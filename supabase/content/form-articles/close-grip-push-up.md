# Close-Grip Push-Up

## Setup
- Start in a plank with hands under your shoulders or slightly narrower — fingers pointing forward.
- Body forms a straight line from head to heels; brace core and squeeze glutes lightly.
- Elbows stay closer to the torso than a standard push-up.
- Scale by elevating hands on a bench or performing kneeling push-ups if needed.

## Execution
- Lower your chest toward the floor while keeping elbows tucked roughly 30–45° from your body.
- Descend until chest nears the floor or as far as shoulder mobility allows with good form.
- Press the floor away to return to the start without sagging hips or piking up.
- Move at a controlled tempo; exhale on the way up.

## Common mistakes
- Elbows flaring straight out — increases shoulder stress and reduces triceps emphasis.
- Hips sagging or piking — breaks the straight-line plank and shifts load to the lower back or shoulders.
- Partial range — stopping high without achieving meaningful elbow flexion.
- Head dropping toward the floor instead of moving the whole torso as one unit.

## Safety notes
- Elevate hands or reduce range if wrist or shoulder pain appears at the bottom.
- Stop if you feel sharp pain in the wrists, shoulders, or lower back.
- Consult a coach for push-up regressions and progressions suited to your level.
