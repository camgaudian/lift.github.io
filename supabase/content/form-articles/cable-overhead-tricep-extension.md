# Cable Overhead Tricep Extension

## Setup
- Attach a rope to a high pulley; face away from the stack and walk forward until tension is on the cable.
- Hold the rope behind your head with elbows bent and pointing forward or slightly out.
- Stagger your stance; brace your core and keep ribs down.
- Start with light weight to find elbow and shoulder comfort in the overhead position.

## Execution
- Extend at the elbows to straighten arms overhead; split the rope ends apart at the top.
- Keep upper arms relatively fixed — only the forearms move.
- Squeeze triceps at full extension without locking elbows aggressively.
- Lower with control until you feel a stretch in the triceps; repeat without flaring ribs.

## Common mistakes
- Elbows drifting wide or forward — reduces triceps tension and stresses the shoulder.
- Arching the lower back to reach full extension — reduce weight or shorten stance.
- Using momentum from a lunge or torso swing.
- Allowing the head to jut forward — keep neck neutral.

## Safety notes
- Reduce load if you feel elbow or shoulder strain in the stretched position.
- Stop if you experience sharp elbow pain during extension.
- See a coach if overhead triceps work consistently aggravates your shoulders or lower back.
