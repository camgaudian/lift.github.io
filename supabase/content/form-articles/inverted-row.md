# Inverted Row

## Setup
- Set a bar in a rack or use TRX rings at roughly waist to chest height when lying underneath.
- Grip the bar with hands shoulder-width apart; body straight from head to heels.
- Heels on the floor with legs extended, or knees bent for an easier variation.
- Retract the shoulder blades slightly before initiating the pull.

## Execution
- Pull your chest toward the bar by driving the elbows back and down.
- Squeeze the shoulder blades together at the top; keep the body in a straight line.
- Lower with control until arms are fully extended without letting the hips sag.
- Exhale on the pull; maintain a braced core throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Hips sagging — reduces back engagement and stresses the lower spine.
- Shrugging the shoulders at the top — limits scapular retraction.
- Partial range of motion — stops short of chest-to-bar or full extension.
- Using momentum by thrusting the hips — reduces upper-back work.

## Safety notes
- Ensure the bar or anchor is secure before loading body weight.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder, elbow, or lower-back pain.
- Elevate the feet or add weight only after mastering a strict bodyweight row.
