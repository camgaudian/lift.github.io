# Hiking

## Setup
- Wear supportive footwear with traction suited to the terrain; adjust pack weight to your fitness and trip length.
- Use trekking poles on uneven or steep trails if they improve balance and reduce knee stress.
- Warm up with easier terrain before climbs; check weather and trail conditions.
- Stand tall with a slight forward lean on inclines; shorten stride length on technical ground.

## Execution
- Walk with a steady rhythm; place the whole foot when possible on rocky or rooted trails.
- On uphill sections, drive through the glutes and keep the chest open; on downhills, soften the knees and land lightly.
- Use poles by pushing back, not planting far ahead, to assist propulsion without hunching.
- Hydrate and fuel regularly; pace for the full distance, not just the first climb.

## Common mistakes
- Overstriding downhill — increases impact on knees and risk of slips.
- Carrying too much weight too soon — leads to poor posture and joint fatigue.
- Ignoring foot placement on uneven ground — raises fall and ankle injury risk.
- Starting too fast without acclimating to elevation or heat.

## Safety notes
- Tell someone your route and expected return time; carry navigation, first aid, and layers appropriate to conditions.
- Stop and seek help for chest pain, severe dizziness, or signs of heat illness or hypothermia.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician before strenuous hiking if you have cardiovascular, balance, or joint limitations.
