# Dumbbell Curl

## Setup
- Stand tall with feet hip-width; hold a dumbbell in each hand at your sides, palms forward.
- Pin the elbows close to the torso; brace the core lightly to limit sway.
- Keep shoulders down and chest lifted; wrists neutral.
- Start with arms fully extended without hyperextending the elbows.

## Execution
- Curl the dumbbells up by flexing the elbows; keep the upper arms stationary.
- Squeeze the biceps at the top without letting the shoulders roll forward.
- Lower with control to full extension; maintain tension through the negative.
- Exhale on the way up if it helps maintain bracing; avoid swinging the torso.

## Common mistakes
- Swinging the hips and shoulders — reduces biceps loading and stresses the lower back.
- Elbows drifting forward — shortens range and shifts work away from the biceps.
- Wrist curling under at the top — unnecessary strain on the wrists and forearms.
- Rushing the negative — wastes time under tension and control.

## Safety notes
- Choose a weight that allows strict form for the target rep range.
- Stop if you feel sharp elbow or forearm pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if curling aggravates existing elbow tendinopathy.
