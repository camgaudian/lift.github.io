# Wide-Grip Pull-Up

## Setup
- Grip the bar overhand with hands wider than shoulder width — typically 1.5× shoulder width or more.
- Hang with arms fully extended; engage the shoulders by depressing the scapulae.
- Cross ankles or keep legs slightly forward to reduce swinging.
- Brace the core before initiating the pull.

## Execution
- Pull the chest toward the bar by driving elbows down and back.
- Lead with the chest; chin clears the bar without craning the neck forward.
- Lower with control to a full hang; reset shoulder tension before the next rep.
- Exhale on the pull; avoid excessive kipping unless performing a kipping variation intentionally.

## Common mistakes
- Half reps — not reaching full extension at the bottom.
- Shrugging shoulders at the top instead of pulling with the lats.
- Excessive swinging and kipping — reduces lat engagement in strict work.
- Gripping too wide for your shoulder mobility — can strain the shoulders.

## Safety notes
- Use a box or band for assistance if you cannot perform controlled strict reps.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder, elbow, or biceps pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if you have shoulder impingement or prior rotator cuff issues.
