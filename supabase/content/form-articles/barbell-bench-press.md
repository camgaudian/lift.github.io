# Barbell Bench Press

## Setup
- Lie on the bench with eyes under the bar; feet flat on the floor.
- Set shoulder blades down and back; maintain a slight natural arch in the lower back.
- Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder width; wrists stacked over elbows.
- Unrack with straight arms and move the bar over mid-chest.

## Execution
- Lower the bar to mid-chest with control; elbows at roughly 45–75° from the torso.
- Touch the chest lightly without bouncing; forearms vertical at the bottom.
- Press up and slightly back toward the rack, exhaling on the way up.
- Keep butt, upper back, and head in contact with the bench throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Flaring elbows to 90° — increases shoulder stress.
- Bouncing the bar off the chest — reduces control and increases injury risk.
- Lifting hips off the bench — reduces stability and can strain the lower back.
- Uneven lockout — often from an off-center grip or uneven bar path.

## Safety notes
- Use safeties or a spotter when lifting heavy or near failure.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder, chest, or wrist pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician for persistent discomfort or prior injuries.
