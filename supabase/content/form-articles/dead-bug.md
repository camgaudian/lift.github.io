# Dead Bug

## Setup
- Lie on your back with arms extended toward the ceiling and knees bent at 90° over the hips.
- Press the lower back gently into the floor; brace the core before moving.
- Keep the head relaxed on the floor or a thin pad; avoid pulling on the neck.

## Execution
- Slowly extend the opposite arm and leg toward the floor while maintaining lower-back contact.
- Exhale as you reach; pause briefly at full extension without arching the spine.
- Return to the start with control; alternate sides for the prescribed reps.
- Move only as far as you can keep the trunk stable and the lower back flat.

## Common mistakes
- Lower back lifting off the floor — reduces core engagement and increases strain.
- Rushing the movement — momentum hides weakness and poor control.
- Holding the breath — brace and exhale through the extension phase.
- Extending both limbs on the same side at once before mastering the opposite pattern.

## Safety notes
- Start with small ranges of motion; progress only when form stays solid.
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back or hip pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if you are recovering from a back or abdominal injury.
