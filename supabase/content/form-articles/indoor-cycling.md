# Indoor Cycling

## Setup
- Adjust the saddle height so your knee has a slight bend at the bottom of the pedal stroke.
- Set handlebar height for a comfortable reach without rounding the lower back excessively.
- Secure feet in pedals or cages; align the ball of the foot over the pedal spindle.
- Set resistance to a moderate level before starting; hydrate and warm up for 3–5 minutes.

## Execution
- Drive the pedal stroke smoothly: push through the front, scrape through the bottom, pull through the back.
- Keep a stable core and relaxed shoulders; elbows slightly bent on the handlebars.
- Match cadence and resistance to the workout goal — steady endurance or structured intervals.
- Cool down with easy spinning and gradually reduce resistance at the end.

## Common mistakes
- Saddle too low or high — causes knee strain and reduces power transfer.
- Gripping the handlebars too tightly — wastes energy and tenses the upper body.
- Riding with zero resistance — reduces training effect and can stress joints.
- Bouncing in the saddle during sprints — often from excessive resistance or poor core control.

## Safety notes
- Stop if you feel sharp knee, hip, or lower-back pain.
- Build duration and intensity gradually, especially if new to cycling.
- Consult a clinician before high-intensity intervals if you have cardiovascular concerns.
