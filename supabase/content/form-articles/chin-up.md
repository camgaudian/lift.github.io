# Chin-Up

## Setup
- Grip the bar with palms facing you, hands shoulder-width or slightly narrower.
- Hang with arms extended; engage shoulders by pulling them slightly down before initiating.
- Cross ankles or keep legs slightly in front of you; brace your core.
- Use a box or band for assistance if you cannot yet perform full reps.

## Execution
- Pull your chest toward the bar by driving elbows down and back.
- Rise until your chin clears the bar or your chest nears it — choose a consistent standard.
- Lower under control to full extension without swinging.
- Reset shoulder position briefly at the bottom if needed before the next rep.

## Common mistakes
- Kipping or swinging — reduces lat and biceps work and increases shoulder stress.
- Chin jutting forward while chest stays far from the bar — shortens range.
- Partial reps at the bottom — limits strength through the hardest range.
- Elbows flaring straight out to the sides — can aggravate the shoulders.

## Safety notes
- Progress with assisted reps, negatives, or holds before forcing max-effort kipping.
- Stop if you feel sharp elbow, shoulder, or biceps pain.
- Consult a coach if pull-up progress stalls or shoulder discomfort persists.
