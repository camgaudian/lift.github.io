# Farmer's Walk

## Setup
- Stand between handles or dumbbells; grip firmly with shoulders packed down and back.
- Brace the core as if preparing for a heavy squat; stand tall before lifting.
- Lift the implements by driving through the floor; avoid rounding the back on the pickup.
- Keep arms straight at your sides; wrists neutral.

## Execution
- Walk with short, controlled steps; maintain an upright posture and level shoulders.
- Breathe steadily; keep the rib cage stacked over the pelvis without leaning side to side.
- Continue for the prescribed distance or time without dropping the implements abruptly.
- Lower the weights with a hip hinge and bent knees, same as a controlled deadlift.

## Common mistakes
- Shrugging the shoulders up — compresses the neck and weakens grip endurance.
- Leaning or listing to one side — often from uneven loads or weak core bracing.
- Taking overly long strides — reduces stability and control under load.
- Letting the chest collapse forward — strains the upper back and reduces capacity.

## Safety notes
- Clear a straight path; tripping under load is a serious fall risk.
- Build distance and weight gradually; grip and trunk endurance limit safe loading.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if you have lower-back or shoulder issues before heavy carries.
