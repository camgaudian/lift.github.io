# Decline Barbell Bench Press

## Setup
- Secure feet under the decline bench pads; hips and upper back in contact with the bench.
- Set shoulder blades down and back; grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder width.
- Unrack with straight arms and position the bar over the lower chest or upper abdomen.
- Use a spotter or safeties when training heavy or near failure.

## Execution
- Lower the bar with control to the lower chest; elbows at roughly 45–60° from the torso.
- Touch lightly without bouncing; forearms vertical at the bottom.
- Press up and slightly back toward the rack; exhale on the concentric phase.
- Maintain foot contact and bench contact throughout the set.

## Common mistakes
- Bouncing the bar off the chest — reduces control and increases injury risk.
- Flaring elbows wide — places extra stress on the shoulders.
- Sliding up the bench during the press — check foot anchoring and bar path.
- Lifting the head off the bench — often from excessive arch or poor setup.

## Safety notes
- Decline pressing increases demand on the shoulders for some lifters; adjust grip and angle as needed.
- Stop immediately for sharp shoulder, chest, or wrist pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if you have a history of shoulder or lower-back issues.
