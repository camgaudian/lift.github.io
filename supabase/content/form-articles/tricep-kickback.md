# Tricep Kickback

## Setup
- Hinge at the hips with a flat back; support one hand on a bench if using a single arm.
- Hold a dumbbell with a neutral grip; upper arm parallel to the floor and pinned to the side.
- Keep the elbow bent at roughly 90° at the start; shoulders square and stable.
- Brace the core to prevent torso rotation during the rep.

## Execution
- Extend the forearm back until the arm is straight; squeeze the triceps at the top.
- Keep the upper arm fixed — only the forearm moves.
- Lower with control to the starting 90° bend without swinging.
- Exhale on the extension; maintain the hip hinge throughout the set.

## Common mistakes
- Dropping the upper arm — reduces triceps isolation.
- Swinging the dumbbell with body momentum — shifts load off the triceps.
- Hyperextending the elbow aggressively — can stress the joint.
- Standing too upright — allows cheating with shoulder movement.

## Safety notes
- Use a weight you can control without swinging the torso.
- Stop if you feel sharp elbow, shoulder, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if elbow discomfort persists across sessions.
