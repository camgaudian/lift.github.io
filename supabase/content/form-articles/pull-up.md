# Pull-Up

## Setup
- Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulders; palms away (overhand) for standard pull-ups.
- Hang with arms extended; engage the core; avoid excessive swinging.
- Retract shoulder blades slightly before initiating the pull.

## Execution
- Pull the chest toward the bar by driving elbows down and back.
- Chin clears the bar or chest reaches bar height at the top.
- Lower with control to full arm extension.
- Maintain a stable torso; minimize kipping unless intentionally training kipping pull-ups.

## Common mistakes
- Partial range at the bottom — limits strength and shoulder health.
- Excessive swinging — reduces lat engagement.
- Craning the neck forward — does not count as clearing the bar; shorten range or use assistance.
- Elbows flaring straight out to the sides — can irritate shoulders.

## Safety notes
- Use a band or assisted machine if you cannot control the full range.
- Stop for sharp shoulder, elbow, or wrist pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if you lack overhead shoulder mobility.
