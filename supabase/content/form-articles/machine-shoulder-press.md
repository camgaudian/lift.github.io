# Machine Shoulder Press

## Setup
- Adjust the seat so the handles start at roughly shoulder height when seated.
- Sit with back against the pad; feet flat on the floor and core braced.
- Grip the handles with wrists stacked over elbows; elbows slightly in front of the body if the machine allows.
- Retract the shoulder blades slightly and set the neck neutral.

## Execution
- Press the handles overhead until arms are extended without locking the elbows harshly.
- Keep the head and upper back in contact with the pad; avoid excessive lower-back arch.
- Lower with control to roughly ear or chin level, depending on shoulder mobility.
- Exhale on the press; drive through the shoulders and triceps evenly.

## Common mistakes
- Overarching the lower back — often from too much weight or poor core bracing.
- Shrugging the shoulders at lockout — reduces stability and irritates the neck.
- Cutting range short at the bottom — limits shoulder strength through a full arc.
- Flaring elbows directly out to the sides — can pinch the shoulder for some lifters.

## Safety notes
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder, neck, or lower-back pain.
- Use a weight you can press without leaning back off the pad.
- Consult a qualified coach if you have a history of shoulder impingement.
