# Sit-Up

## Setup
- Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart.
- Place hands lightly behind the head or crossed on the chest; do not pull on the neck.
- Brace the core and press the lower back gently toward the floor before starting.
- Keep the chin slightly tucked as if holding a tennis ball under it.

## Execution
- Curl the torso up by flexing the spine; lead with the chest, not the head.
- Rise until the shoulder blades leave the floor or you reach a controlled peak contraction.
- Lower slowly to the start without collapsing; maintain tension through the abs.
- Exhale on the way up; inhale on the descent.

## Common mistakes
- Yanking the head forward with the hands — strains the neck and reduces ab work.
- Using hip flexor momentum to swing up — keep the movement trunk-focused.
- Arching the lower back excessively at the bottom — reset bracing each rep.
- Holding breath through the entire rep — breathe steadily.

## Safety notes
- Switch to a crunch or dead bug if you feel lower-back discomfort during full sit-ups.
- Avoid high-rep sit-ups if you have disc-related symptoms unless cleared by a clinician.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if sit-ups consistently provoke neck or back pain.
