# Cable Fly

## Setup
- Set pulleys at shoulder height (or slightly above for low-to-high variation).
- Grab handles and step forward into a staggered stance; slight bend in elbows.
- Lean forward slightly with a braced core; shoulders down and back.
- Start with arms wide and a stretch across the chest without forcing extreme range.

## Execution
- Bring handles together in a hugging arc, squeezing chest at the center.
- Keep the elbow angle fixed — movement comes from the shoulder joint.
- Pause briefly at peak contraction without letting shoulders shrug up.
- Open arms under control until you feel a chest stretch; do not bounce.

## Common mistakes
- Turning the fly into a press by bending and extending elbows excessively.
- Overstretching at the bottom — stresses the shoulder capsule.
- Using momentum from the torso swing to close the handles.
- Setting pulleys at the wrong height for your target angle — adjust for upper vs lower chest emphasis.

## Safety notes
- Use a weight you can control through the full arc on every rep.
- Stop if you feel sharp anterior shoulder or pec pain.
- See a coach if shoulder discomfort appears whenever you train chest fly patterns.
