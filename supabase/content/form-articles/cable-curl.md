# Cable Curl

## Setup
- Attach a straight bar or EZ bar to a low pulley.
- Stand facing the stack, feet hip-width apart; grip the bar underhand at shoulder width.
- Keep elbows pinned to your sides; brace your core and stand tall.
- Take a small step back so the weight does not rest at the bottom between reps.

## Execution
- Curl the bar toward your shoulders by flexing at the elbows only.
- Squeeze biceps at the top without letting shoulders roll forward.
- Lower under control until arms are fully extended.
- Maintain constant tension — avoid letting the stack slam at the bottom.

## Common mistakes
- Leaning back to cheat the weight up — reduces biceps load and stresses the lower back.
- Elbows drifting forward during the curl.
- Using momentum from a hip thrust at the start of each rep.
- Gripping too wide or too narrow for your shoulder comfort.

## Safety notes
- Select a load that allows strict reps through your intended range.
- Stop if you feel sharp elbow or wrist pain.
- See a coach to compare cable curls with free-weight options if elbow discomfort persists.
