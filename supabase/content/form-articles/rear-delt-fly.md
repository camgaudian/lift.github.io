# Rear Delt Fly

## Setup
- Hold dumbbells or use a cable or machine; hinge at the hips until the torso is roughly parallel to the floor.
- Let arms hang beneath the shoulders with a slight bend in the elbows.
- Brace the core; keep the neck neutral and back flat.

## Execution
- Raise the arms out to the sides in a wide arc, leading with the elbows or pinkies.
- Squeeze the rear delts at the top; avoid shrugging the traps excessively.
- Lower with control to the starting position; maintain the hip hinge throughout.
- Keep the torso still; movement comes only from the shoulder joint.

## Common mistakes
- Using too much weight and swinging — prioritize control and rear-delt feel.
- Shrugging traps to lift the weight — focus on moving through the shoulders, not the neck.
- Standing too upright — a proper hinge targets the rear delts more effectively.
- Bending and extending the elbows — keep a fixed soft bend throughout.

## Safety notes
- Start light; rear delts respond better to moderate load and higher reps.
- Stop for sharp shoulder or upper-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician for prior shoulder impingement or rotator cuff issues.
