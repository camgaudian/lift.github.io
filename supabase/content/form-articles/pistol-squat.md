# Pistol Squat

## Setup
- Stand on one leg; extend the free leg in front with toes up or slightly out.
- Arms reach forward for balance; standing foot stays flat with weight through mid-foot and heel.
- Brace the core; keep the chest up and hips square to the front.

## Execution
- Sit back and down on the working leg; knee tracks over the toes without collapsing inward.
- Descend as low as control and mobility allow; free leg stays off the floor.
- Drive through the heel to stand; lock out the hip and knee at the top.
- Complete all reps on one leg before switching sides.

## Common mistakes
- Knee caving inward — actively push the knee out over the toes.
- Heel lifting off the floor — reduce depth or use a counterweight until mobility improves.
- Rounding the lower back at the bottom — stop at the depth you can maintain a neutral spine.
- Dropping into the bottom — control the descent; avoid bouncing out of the hole.

## Safety notes
- Progress with box pistols, assisted holds, or counterbalance before full range.
- Stop for sharp knee, ankle, or hip pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if you have prior knee or ankle injuries.
