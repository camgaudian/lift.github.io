# Hip Abduction

## Setup
- On a seated abduction machine, sit with back against the pad and feet on the foot rests.
- Place the outer thighs against the pads; adjust seat so knees align with the machine axis.
- Brace the core lightly; hold the handles for stability without pulling the torso forward.
- Start with legs together and tension on the stack.

## Execution
- Press the legs apart by driving the knees outward through the pads.
- Open to a comfortable range without rocking the pelvis or arching the lower back.
- Squeeze the glutes at the end range; pause briefly.
- Return with control to the start without letting the weight slam.

## Common mistakes
- Leaning forward or arching off the seat — reduces glute medius isolation.
- Using momentum to snap the legs apart — sacrifices control and joint safety.
- Opening farther than active range allows — shifts stress to the lower back and hips.
- Holding the breath — breathe steadily through each rep.

## Safety notes
- Use moderate loads; this is an accessory movement for hip stability, not maximal strength testing.
- Stop for sharp outer-hip or groin pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if abduction work aggravates hip labral or IT band symptoms.
