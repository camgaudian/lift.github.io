# Mountain Climber

## Setup
- Start in a high plank with hands under shoulders and body in a straight line from head to heels.
- Brace the core and set the feet hip-width apart for a stable base.
- Keep shoulders stacked over wrists; gaze slightly ahead of the hands.
- Begin in a slow, controlled rhythm before increasing speed.

## Execution
- Drive one knee toward the chest while keeping the hips level and the opposite leg extended.
- Return that foot and switch legs in a running motion, alternating sides each rep.
- Maintain a braced core so the lower back does not sag or pike upward.
- Breathe steadily; increase pace only while form stays intact.

## Common mistakes
- Hips bouncing up and down — reduces core engagement and shifts load to the lower back.
- Hands drifting forward of the shoulders — increases wrist and shoulder strain.
- Collapsing through the upper back — weak plank position limits control.
- Going too fast too soon — form breaks down before the core is ready.

## Safety notes
- Stop if you feel sharp wrist, shoulder, or lower-back pain.
- Scale by slowing down or elevating the hands on a bench.
- Build duration gradually to avoid overuse soreness in the wrists and hip flexors.
