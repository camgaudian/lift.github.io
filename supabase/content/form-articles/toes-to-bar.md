# Toes to Bar

## Setup
- Hang from a pull-up bar with an overhand grip at shoulder width or slightly wider.
- Engage the shoulders by depressing the scapulae — do not hang passively.
- Start with legs together and arms straight; brace the core before initiating.
- Use chalk or grip aids if needed for secure hanging.

## Execution
- Initiate by posteriorly tilting the pelvis and lifting the legs with control.
- Raise toes to touch the bar; keep arms straight throughout.
- Lower legs with control to avoid swinging; reset tension before the next rep.
- Exhale on the lift; maintain active shoulders for the entire set.

## Common mistakes
- Excessive kipping and swinging — reduces core work and can strain the shoulders.
- Dropping legs uncontrolled — creates momentum that is hard to manage.
- Passive dead hang — increases shoulder stress over time.
- Using hip flexors only without core engagement — limits control and range.

## Safety notes
- Build hanging strength and shoulder stability before attempting strict reps.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder, elbow, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if you have shoulder impingement or prior grip or back issues.
