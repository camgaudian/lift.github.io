# Calf Raise

## Setup
- Stand on a calf-raise block or step with the balls of your feet on the edge and heels hanging off.
- Use a smith machine, rack, or dumbbells for load — start bodyweight to learn balance.
- Hold a support for balance if needed; feet hip-width apart or slightly turned out.
- Set ankles in neutral alignment before starting.

## Execution
- Rise onto your toes as high as possible, pushing through the big toe.
- Pause briefly at the top; squeeze calves without bouncing.
- Lower heels below the step level for a full stretch if mobility allows.
- Move through a controlled tempo — avoid dropping into the bottom.

## Common mistakes
- Bouncing at the bottom — uses elasticity instead of muscle work and stresses the Achilles.
- Partial range — stopping before a full stretch or peak contraction.
- Letting ankles roll inward or outward at the top.
- Bending knees excessively — shifts work toward the soleus; fine if intentional, but know the difference.

## Safety notes
- Progress load gradually; calves respond well to volume but tolerate poor loading poorly at first.
- Stop if you feel sharp Achilles or calf pain.
- Consult a coach if you have a history of Achilles tendinopathy before heavy loaded raises.
