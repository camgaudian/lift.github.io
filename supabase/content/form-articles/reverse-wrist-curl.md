# Reverse Wrist Curl

## Setup
- Sit on a bench; forearms rest on the thighs or a flat bench, wrists hanging just past the knees.
- Hold a barbell or dumbbells with an overhand grip; palms face down.
- Keep the forearms pinned down; only the wrists move during the exercise.

## Execution
- Extend the wrists to lower the weight toward the floor; feel a stretch in the forearm extensors.
- Curl the wrists upward as high as comfortable; squeeze at the top.
- Lower with control; avoid bouncing at the bottom of the range.
- Move through a full but pain-free range of motion.

## Common mistakes
- Lifting the forearms off the thighs — only the wrists should move.
- Using too much weight — extensors are small muscles; start very light.
- Rushing reps — use a slow, controlled tempo for better results.
- Gripping too tightly — hold the weight firmly without crushing the bar.

## Safety notes
- Use light weight; this exercise targets small muscles and tendons.
- Stop for sharp wrist, elbow, or forearm pain.
- Consult a qualified clinician if you have prior wrist tendinopathy or carpal tunnel symptoms.
