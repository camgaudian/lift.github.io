# Incline Dumbbell Curl

## Setup
- Set a bench to 45–60° incline; sit back with shoulders against the pad.
- Hold a dumbbell in each hand at arm's length, palms facing forward or slightly inward.
- Keep elbows close to your sides and allow arms to hang straight down behind the torso.
- Brace lightly through the core so you do not arch excessively off the bench.

## Execution
- Curl the dumbbells up by flexing the elbows; keep upper arms stationary.
- Squeeze the biceps at the top without letting the shoulders roll forward.
- Lower with control to full extension without locking out aggressively.
- Alternate or curl both arms together as programmed; maintain the same elbow position each rep.

## Common mistakes
- Swinging the torso — uses momentum instead of the biceps.
- Elbows drifting forward — shortens the range and reduces stretch at the bottom.
- Shrugging the shoulders — takes tension off the biceps.
- Cutting the range short at the bottom — reduces the incline stretch benefit.

## Safety notes
- Use a weight you can control through the full range.
- Stop if you feel sharp elbow or shoulder pain.
- Avoid hyperextending the elbows at the bottom if you have elbow sensitivity.
