# Concentration Curl

## Setup
- Sit on a bench with legs spread; brace the working arm's triceps against the inside of the same-side thigh.
- Hold a dumbbell with an underhand grip; arm hangs straight toward the floor.
- Keep torso still and core braced; non-working hand can rest on the other knee for stability.
- Choose a moderate weight — strict form is the point of this exercise.

## Execution
- Curl the dumbbell toward your shoulder by flexing only at the elbow.
- Keep the upper arm pinned against the thigh throughout the rep.
- Squeeze at the top without rotating the shoulder forward.
- Lower slowly to full extension; avoid swinging the weight down.

## Common mistakes
- Lifting the elbow off the thigh to cheat the weight up.
- Leaning the torso back as you curl.
- Rushing reps and using momentum at the bottom.
- Incomplete extension — shortens range and reduces time under tension.

## Safety notes
- Control the weight on the way down to protect the elbow at full extension.
- Stop if you feel sharp elbow or wrist pain.
- See a coach if isolation curls consistently irritate your elbows — grip and load may need adjustment.
