# Good Morning

## Setup
- Position a barbell across the upper back as in a back squat; feet hip- to shoulder-width.
- Brace the core hard; unlock the knees slightly but keep them mostly fixed.
- Hinge at the hips by pushing them back; maintain a neutral spine from head to tailbone.
- Start with light weight until the hip hinge pattern is consistent.

## Execution
- Lower the torso forward by hinging at the hips until you feel a strong hamstring stretch.
- Keep the bar over the mid-foot; shins stay relatively vertical.
- Drive the hips forward to return to standing; squeeze the glutes at the top without hyperextending.
- Control the descent; do not drop into the bottom position.

## Common mistakes
- Rounding the lower back — high injury risk; reduce range or load immediately.
- Bending the knees excessively — turns the movement into a squat hinge hybrid.
- Bar drifting forward of the toes — increases spinal shear forces.
- Using too much weight too soon — good mornings demand excellent bracing and experience.

## Safety notes
- Treat this as an advanced hinge; many lifters substitute Romanian deadlifts until ready.
- Stop immediately for sharp lower-back or hamstring pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician before good mornings if you have a history of back issues.
