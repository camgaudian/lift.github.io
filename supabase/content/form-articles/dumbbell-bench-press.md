# Dumbbell Bench Press

## Setup
- Lie on a flat bench with a dumbbell in each hand; feet flat on the floor.
- Set shoulder blades down and back; dumbbells at chest level with palms facing forward or slightly angled.
- Wrists neutral and stacked over elbows; maintain light contact between head, upper back, and hips and the bench.
- Press the dumbbells up together to the start position with control.

## Execution
- Lower the dumbbells until elbows reach roughly bench height or a comfortable stretch at the chest.
- Keep forearms vertical at the bottom; elbows at about 45–75° from the torso.
- Press up and slightly inward without clanking the dumbbells at the top.
- Exhale on the press; maintain stable shoulder position throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Dropping the elbows too low — increases shoulder strain.
- Uneven arm paths — often from one side dominating; match range and tempo.
- Over-arching the lower back and lifting the rib cage off the bench — reduces stability.
- Using excessive dumbbell angle that torques the wrists — keep wrists neutral.

## Safety notes
- Use a spotter or controlled kick-up when handling heavy dumbbells.
- Stop for sharp shoulder, chest, or wrist pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician for persistent pressing discomfort.
