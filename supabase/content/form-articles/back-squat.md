# Back Squat

## Setup
- Set the bar on upper traps (high bar) or rear delts (low bar); hands evenly spaced.
- Stand with feet shoulder-width or slightly wider; toes turned out 15–30°.
- Brace the core; take a breath and unrack; step back into position.

## Execution
- Sit hips back and down while keeping the chest up and knees tracking over toes.
- Descend until hip crease is at or below knee level (if mobility allows).
- Drive through mid-foot to stand; exhale near the top without losing brace.
- Keep the bar over mid-foot throughout the rep.

## Common mistakes
- Knees caving inward — often from weak glutes or poor foot pressure.
- Excessive forward lean — can shift load to the lower back.
- Rising hips first out of the hole — turns the squat into a good morning.
- Heels lifting — reduces stability; adjust stance or ankle mobility work.

## Safety notes
- Use rack safeties set just below your lowest squat depth.
- Stop if you feel sharp knee, hip, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach for form checks before adding significant load.
