# Hollow Body Hold

## Setup
- Lie on your back with arms extended overhead and legs straight.
- Press your lower back firmly into the floor; engage your abs before moving.
- Lift shoulders and legs a few inches off the ground, keeping legs together and toes pointed.
- Hold your chin slightly tucked so your neck stays neutral.

## Execution
- Maintain the hollow shape: ribs down, pelvis slightly posteriorly tilted, glutes lightly engaged.
- Breathe shallowly through the diaphragm without letting your lower back arch off the floor.
- Hold for the prescribed time; reduce hold duration or raise limbs slightly if form breaks down.
- Rest briefly between sets and reset the lower-back contact before each hold.

## Common mistakes
- Lower back peeling off the floor — reduces core tension and strains the spine.
- Holding the breath — creates unnecessary tension; breathe steadily instead.
- Lifting the head too high — strains the neck; keep a small gap under the chin.
- Legs too low for current strength — causes the back to lift; raise legs or bend knees slightly.

## Safety notes
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back or neck pain.
- Build up hold time gradually; quality beats duration.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if you have a history of lower-back issues.
