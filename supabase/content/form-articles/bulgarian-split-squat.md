# Bulgarian Split Squat

## Setup
- Stand two to three feet in front of a bench; place the top of your rear foot on the bench behind you.
- Keep most of your weight on the front foot; front knee stacked over mid-foot.
- Hold dumbbells at your sides or a barbell on your back — start bodyweight to learn balance.
- Brace your core and square your hips forward.

## Execution
- Lower straight down until the front thigh is roughly parallel to the floor or as mobility allows.
- Keep the front knee tracking over the second toe; torso slightly forward is acceptable.
- Drive through the front heel to stand; minimize pushing off the back foot.
- Complete all reps on one leg before switching.

## Common mistakes
- Front foot too close to the bench — increases knee shear and reduces stability.
- Rear foot turned awkwardly on the bench — causes hip rotation and balance issues.
- Bouncing at the bottom — reduces control and joint safety.
- Leaning excessively sideways — often from a weight that is too heavy.

## Safety notes
- Use a support for balance when learning the movement.
- Stop if you feel sharp front-knee or hip flexor pain.
- Consult a coach if you cannot keep the front knee aligned during the descent.
