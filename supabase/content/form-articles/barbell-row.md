# Barbell Row

## Setup
- Hinge at the hips with a flat or slightly neutral spine; torso roughly 30–45° to the floor.
- Grip the bar shoulder-width or slightly wider; arms extended toward the floor.
- Brace the core; knees slightly bent; weight balanced over mid-foot.

## Execution
- Pull the bar toward the lower ribs or upper abdomen.
- Lead with the elbows; squeeze shoulder blades together at the top.
- Lower with control until arms are extended without rounding the back.
- Keep the torso angle stable; avoid using momentum from the hips.

## Common mistakes
- Standing too upright — shifts work away from the back.
- Rounding the lower back — reduce weight and improve hinge position.
- Shrugging at the top — overuses traps instead of mid-back.
- Short range — bar should reach the torso each rep.

## Safety notes
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back pain.
- Use straps only when grip limits training, not to mask form breakdown.
- Consult a qualified coach for persistent discomfort.
