# Thruster

## Setup
- Set the barbell in a front-rack position — bar on front delts, elbows high, hands outside shoulders.
- Stand with feet shoulder-width; toes slightly out; core braced.
- Take a breath and unrack; step back into a stable stance.
- Keep the chest up and elbows parallel to the floor before squatting.

## Execution
- Squat to at least parallel while keeping the bar on the shoulders.
- Drive explosively through the legs; use leg momentum to press the bar overhead.
- Lock out the arms at the top with the bar over mid-foot.
- Lower the bar back to the front rack with control and descend into the next rep.

## Common mistakes
- Pressing with arms only — wastes leg drive and limits load.
- Elbows dropping in the front rack — bar rolls forward and stresses the wrists.
- Pressing the bar forward instead of straight up — poor bar path and shoulder strain.
- Shallow squats — reduces power transfer into the press.

## Safety notes
- Use bumper plates and a clear area when dropping failed reps from overhead.
- Stop if you feel sharp knee, shoulder, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach for front-rack and overhead form before adding significant load.
