# Sumo Squat

## Setup
- Stand with feet wider than shoulder width; toes turned out 30–45°.
- Hold a dumbbell vertically at chest height or let it hang between the legs.
- Brace the core; keep the chest up and shoulders back.
- Distribute weight evenly across both feet before descending.

## Execution
- Sit hips straight down between the legs; knees track over the toes.
- Descend until thighs are parallel or as deep as mobility allows.
- Drive through mid-foot and heels to stand; squeeze glutes at the top.
- Keep the torso as upright as possible throughout the rep.

## Common mistakes
- Knees collapsing inward — push knees out in line with the toes.
- Rising onto the toes — often from ankle mobility limits or excessive forward lean.
- Letting the dumbbell pull the torso forward — reduce load or shorten range.
- Shallow depth — limits glute and adductor engagement.

## Safety notes
- Use a stable surface; avoid slippery flooring with heavy dumbbells.
- Stop if you feel sharp hip, groin, or knee pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if hip or ankle mobility limits safe depth.
