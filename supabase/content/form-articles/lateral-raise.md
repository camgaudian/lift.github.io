# Lateral Raise

## Setup
- Stand with feet hip-width apart or sit upright on a bench; hold dumbbells at your sides.
- Maintain a slight bend in the elbows that stays fixed throughout the set.
- Brace the core; shoulders down and chest up.
- Start with the weights just in front of or beside the thighs.

## Execution
- Raise the arms out to the sides until they reach roughly shoulder height.
- Lead with the elbows; pinkies slightly higher than thumbs if comfortable for your shoulders.
- Pause briefly at the top without shrugging the shoulders toward the ears.
- Lower with control to the start without swinging the torso.

## Common mistakes
- Using momentum by leaning or thrusting the hips — reduces delt isolation.
- Raising above shoulder height — increases impingement risk for many lifters.
- Shrugging at the top — shifts work to the traps.
- Straightening the elbows during the lift — turns the raise into a different movement pattern.

## Safety notes
- Use lighter weights than you think you need; this is an isolation exercise.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder pain, especially on the way up.
- Reduce range or use a slight forward lean if you feel pinching in the shoulder joint.
