# Jump Rope

## Setup
- Choose a rope length so handles reach roughly mid-chest when standing on the center of the rope.
- Stand tall with feet hip-width apart; elbows close to the ribs, wrists at hip height.
- Hold the handles lightly; rotate from the wrists, not the shoulders.
- Start on a forgiving surface with enough overhead clearance.

## Execution
- Jump just high enough for the rope to pass under the feet — typically 1–2 inches off the ground.
- Land softly on the balls of the feet with knees slightly bent.
- Keep a steady rhythm; start slow and build cadence as coordination improves.
- Breathe steadily; stay relaxed through the shoulders and grip.

## Common mistakes
- Jumping too high — wastes energy and increases landing impact.
- Swinging the arms from the shoulders — slows the rope and causes tripping.
- Landing flat-footed or with locked knees — increases joint stress.
- Rope too long or short — causes poor timing and excessive arm movement.

## Safety notes
- Stop if you feel sharp ankle, knee, or shin pain.
- Build volume gradually to avoid overuse soreness in the calves and feet.
- Use a mat or softer surface when possible to reduce impact.
