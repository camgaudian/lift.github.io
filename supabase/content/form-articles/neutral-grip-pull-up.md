# Neutral-Grip Pull-Up

## Setup
- Grip parallel handles or a bar with palms facing each other, hands shoulder-width apart.
- Hang with arms fully extended; engage the shoulders slightly so you are not passive in the hang.
- Cross ankles or keep legs slightly in front of the body for stability.
- Retract the shoulder blades before initiating the pull.

## Execution
- Pull your chest toward the handles by driving the elbows down and back.
- Squeeze the lats and upper back at the top; chin clears the bar or handles if mobility allows.
- Lower with control to full extension without swinging or kipping unless programmed.
- Exhale on the pull; maintain a braced core throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Shrugging the shoulders at the bottom — increases neck and shoulder strain.
- Kipping or swinging for reps — reduces pulling strength development.
- Partial range at the top or bottom — limits lat and scapular training.
- Craning the neck forward to clear the bar — strains the cervical spine.

## Safety notes
- Use a band or assisted machine if you cannot control the full range.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder, elbow, or wrist pain.
- Build volume gradually to protect the elbows and biceps tendons.
