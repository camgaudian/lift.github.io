# Machine Chest Press

## Setup
- Adjust the seat height so the handles align with mid-chest when seated.
- Sit with back flat against the pad; feet flat on the floor.
- Grip the handles with wrists stacked over elbows; retract the shoulder blades lightly.
- Set a comfortable starting position with elbows slightly behind or in line with the torso.

## Execution
- Press the handles forward until arms are extended without locking the elbows aggressively.
- Squeeze the chest at the top; keep the shoulder blades pinned to the pad.
- Return with control until you feel a stretch across the chest without letting the shoulders roll forward.
- Exhale on the press; maintain head contact with the pad.

## Common mistakes
- Seat too low or high — shifts stress to the shoulders instead of the chest.
- Shrugging the shoulders on the press — reduces chest engagement.
- Bouncing out of the bottom — increases shoulder and pec strain.
- Arching the lower back off the pad — compromises stability.

## Safety notes
- Start with a light weight to confirm handle path and seat position.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder, chest, or wrist pain.
- Use a full range you can control without shoulder discomfort at the bottom.
