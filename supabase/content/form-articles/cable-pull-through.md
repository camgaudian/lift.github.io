# Cable Pull-Through

## Setup
- Attach a rope to a low pulley; face away from the stack with the rope between your legs.
- Walk forward until there is tension; feet hip- to shoulder-width apart.
- Hinge at the hips with a soft knee bend; grip the rope between your legs with arms extended.
- Keep a neutral spine and chest proud at the bottom of the hinge.

## Execution
- Drive hips forward by squeezing glutes; stand tall without hyperextending the lower back.
- Arms stay straight — they transmit force from the hips, not pull with the back.
- Control the return by pushing hips back; feel tension in hamstrings and glutes.
- Maintain a consistent foot pressure through mid-foot and heels.

## Common mistakes
- Squatting the movement — excessive knee bend turns it into a leg press pattern.
- Rounding the upper back at the bottom of the hinge.
- Pulling the rope with bent arms and lats — the glutes should finish the rep.
- Snapping hips forward with lumbar hyperextension at lockout.
- Using too heavy a load before mastering the hip hinge pattern.

## Safety notes
- Master the hip hinge with light weight before progressing.
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back or hamstring pain.
- Consult a coach to compare pull-throughs with Romanian deadlifts for your training goals.
