# Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

## Setup
- Stand on one leg; hold a dumbbell or kettlebell in the opposite hand or both hands.
- Soft bend in the standing knee; core braced, shoulders back.
- Free leg extends behind as a counterbalance; hips stay square to the floor.

## Execution
- Hinge at the hip on the standing leg; lower the weight toward the floor along the front of the shin.
- Keep the back flat and the free leg moving in line with the torso; stop when hamstrings limit range.
- Drive the hip forward to return to standing; squeeze the glute at the top.
- Complete all reps on one leg before switching sides.

## Common mistakes
- Rounding the lower back — reduce range or weight until a flat spine is maintained.
- Rotating the hips open — keep both hip bones facing the floor throughout.
- Bending the standing knee excessively — use a soft, not deep, knee bend.
- Loss of balance — use a wall or dowel for support while learning the pattern.

## Safety notes
- Progress from bodyweight or supported reps before adding load.
- Stop for sharp hamstring, hip, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician for prior hamstring or lower-back injuries.
