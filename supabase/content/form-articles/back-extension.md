# Back Extension

## Setup
- Position yourself on the back-extension bench with hips at the pad edge and feet secured.
- Cross arms over chest or hold a weight plate at chest level for added load.
- Start with a neutral spine — avoid hyperextending at the top before you begin.
- Set the pad height so your hips can hinge freely without the femur cutting off movement.

## Execution
- Lower your torso by hinging at the hips while keeping a flat back.
- Descend until you feel a stretch in the hamstrings and glutes, not a pinch in the lower back.
- Raise by squeezing glutes and extending the hips; stop when your body forms a straight line.
- Pause briefly at the top without overarching the lumbar spine.

## Common mistakes
- Hyperextending at the top — compresses lumbar vertebrae instead of training glutes and erectors.
- Using a fast, bouncing motion at the bottom — reduces control and increases injury risk.
- Leading with the head and neck instead of hinging from the hips.
- Setting the pad too high — limits hip hinge and shifts load to the lower back.

## Safety notes
- Keep reps controlled; this is not a ballistic movement.
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back pain rather than muscular fatigue.
- See a coach if you cannot maintain a neutral spine throughout the range of motion.
