# Incline Barbell Bench Press

## Setup
- Set the bench to 30–45°; lie back with eyes under the bar and feet flat on the floor.
- Retract and depress the shoulder blades; maintain a slight natural arch in the lower back.
- Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder width; wrists stacked over elbows.
- Unrack with straight arms and position the bar over the upper chest or clavicle area.

## Execution
- Lower the bar to the upper chest with control; elbows at roughly 45–60° from the torso.
- Touch lightly without bouncing; forearms vertical at the bottom.
- Press up and slightly back toward the rack, exhaling on the way up.
- Keep butt, upper back, and head in contact with the bench throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Setting the incline too steep — shifts work to the shoulders and reduces chest engagement.
- Flaring elbows to 90° — increases shoulder stress.
- Bouncing the bar off the chest — reduces control and increases injury risk.
- Drifting the bar toward the belly — changes the angle and stresses the shoulders.

## Safety notes
- Use a spotter or safety arms when lifting heavy or near failure.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder, chest, or wrist pain.
- Consult a qualified coach for form checks before adding significant load.
