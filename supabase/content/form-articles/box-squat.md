# Box Squat

## Setup
- Set a box or bench at a height that allows a parallel or slightly below-parallel squat for your mobility.
- Position the bar as you would for a back squat; unrack and step back to the box.
- Stand with your normal squat stance; brace before each rep.
- Sit back toward the box — do not plop down.

## Execution
- Break at hips and knees together while keeping chest up and knees tracking over toes.
- Touch the box lightly with your glutes — maintain tension; do not fully relax on the box.
- Pause briefly without losing brace, then drive through mid-foot to stand.
- Keep the bar over mid-foot throughout the rep.

## Common mistakes
- Collapsing onto the box — removes tension and can stress the lower back on the rebound.
- Rocking forward to stand — often from a box that is too low or weak quad drive.
- Knees caving on the ascent — check foot pressure and glute engagement.
- Using the box as a depth cheat without controlling the descent.

## Safety notes
- Set rack safeties even when using a box.
- Stop if you feel sharp knee, hip, or lower-back pain.
- See a coach to align box height and bar position with your squat goals.
