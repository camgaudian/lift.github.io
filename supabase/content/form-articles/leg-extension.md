# Leg Extension

## Setup
- Adjust the seat so your knees align with the machine pivot and the pad sits on the lower shin.
- Sit with back against the pad; grip the handles and set feet under the roller pad.
- Start with knees bent roughly 90° and hips fully seated in the chair.
- Point toes slightly up or straight ahead based on comfort.

## Execution
- Extend the knees to straighten the legs; squeeze the quads at the top without locking out harshly.
- Lower with control to the start position without letting the weight stack slam.
- Keep the hips and lower back pressed into the seat throughout.
- Exhale on the extension; use a smooth, deliberate tempo.

## Common mistakes
- Locking the knees aggressively at the top — increases joint stress.
- Lifting the hips off the seat to help the weight up — reduces quad isolation.
- Using excessive load with a swinging kick — stresses the knee joint.
- Partial range at the bottom — reduces time under tension.

## Safety notes
- Use moderate loads; leg extensions place shear force on the knee.
- Stop if you feel sharp or grinding knee pain.
- Consult a clinician before heavy leg extensions if you have knee ligament or patellar issues.
