# Cable Lateral Raise

## Setup
- Attach a handle to the low pulley; stand sideways to the stack with the working arm farthest from the machine.
- Grip the handle with a neutral or slight pronated grip; working arm starts at your side.
- Stand tall with a braced core; non-working hand can hold the stack for stability.
- Choose a light weight — lateral raises reward control over load.

## Execution
- Raise your arm out to the side until it reaches roughly shoulder height.
- Lead with the elbow; keep a soft bend in the arm throughout.
- Pause briefly at the top without shrugging the trap up to your ear.
- Lower slowly; resist the cable pulling your arm down abruptly.

## Common mistakes
- Raising above shoulder height — increases impingement risk without extra delt benefit.
- Shrugging or leaning away from the stack to cheat the weight up.
- Using hip sway or trunk rotation to initiate the rep.
- Gripping too heavy and turning the raise into a upright row pattern.

## Safety notes
- Keep thumbs slightly up or neutral if standard raises bother your shoulders.
- Stop if you feel pinching on the top or front of the shoulder.
- Consult a coach for shoulder-friendly variations if raises consistently cause discomfort.
