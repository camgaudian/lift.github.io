# Arnold Press

## Setup
- Sit on a bench with back support or stand with feet hip-width apart.
- Hold dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing you, elbows in front of the torso.
- Brace your core; keep wrists stacked over forearms.
- Set shoulder blades lightly down and back without over-arching the lower back.

## Execution
- Press upward while rotating palms to face forward at the top.
- Finish with arms extended overhead, biceps near ears, without flaring ribs.
- Lower with control, reversing the rotation so palms face you at the bottom.
- Keep the movement smooth — avoid bouncing at the bottom.

## Common mistakes
- Excessive lower-back arch to get weight overhead — reduce load or sit with back support.
- Pressing the dumbbells too far in front of the body — shifts stress away from delts.
- Rushing the rotation — increases shoulder strain.
- Using momentum from the legs when standing — keep lower body stable.

## Safety notes
- Use a weight you can control through full rotation without shoulder pinching.
- Stop if you feel sharp pain in the shoulder or neck.
- Consult a coach if overhead pressing consistently aggravates your shoulders.
