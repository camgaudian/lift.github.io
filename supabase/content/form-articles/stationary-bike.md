# Stationary Bike

## Setup
- Adjust the seat height so the knee has a slight bend at the bottom of the pedal stroke.
- Set handlebar height for a comfortable, neutral spine — not hunched or over-extended.
- Secure feet in pedals or straps; align the ball of the foot over the pedal spindle.
- Select resistance and program settings appropriate to your session goal.

## Execution
- Pedal with a smooth circular stroke; push down and pull up through the full rotation.
- Keep knees tracking over the feet; avoid bowing knees outward or inward.
- Maintain an upright or slightly forward torso; relax shoulders away from the ears.
- Adjust cadence and resistance gradually; breathe steadily throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Seat too low — increases knee stress and reduces power output.
- Seat too high — causes hip rocking and lower-back strain.
- Gripping handlebars too tightly — creates upper-body tension.
- Starting at maximum resistance — leads to poor form and early fatigue.

## Safety notes
- Stop if you feel chest pain, dizziness, or sharp knee or hip pain.
- Hydrate during longer sessions; cool down with easy pedaling afterward.
- Consult a qualified clinician before intense cycling if you have cardiovascular conditions.
