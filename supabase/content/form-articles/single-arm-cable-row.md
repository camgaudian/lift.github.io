# Single-Arm Cable Row

## Setup
- Attach a single handle to a low cable; stand or kneel facing the stack with feet staggered.
- Grab the handle with one hand; arm extended, torso hinged slightly forward, core braced.
- Keep the shoulder down; opposite hand can rest on the thigh or knee for support.

## Execution
- Pull the handle toward the lower ribs or hip; elbow drives back close to the body.
- Squeeze the shoulder blade toward the spine at the end of the pull.
- Extend the arm with control; allow a slight stretch without twisting the torso.
- Complete all reps on one side, then switch arms.

## Common mistakes
- Rotating the torso to pull the weight — keep the chest facing forward.
- Shrugging the working shoulder — depress the shoulder and lead with the elbow.
- Using momentum — reduce weight and focus on a controlled pull and release.
- Elbow flaring wide — keep the elbow tracking back along the rib cage.

## Safety notes
- Choose a load that allows strict form without torso rotation.
- Stop for sharp lower-back, shoulder, or biceps pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if you have prior lower-back or shoulder injuries.
