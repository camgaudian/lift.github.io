# Dumbbell Shoulder Press

## Setup
- Sit on a bench with back support or stand with feet hip-width; hold dumbbells at shoulder height.
- Palms face forward or slightly angled; wrists stacked over elbows.
- Set ribs down and brace the core; avoid excessive lower-back arch when seated.
- Start with elbows slightly in front of the body, not flared straight out.

## Execution
- Press the dumbbells overhead until arms are extended without aggressive locking.
- Move the weights slightly inward at the top so they finish over the shoulders, not in front of the face.
- Lower with control to shoulder level or just below the chin line.
- Keep the head neutral; do not jut the chin forward as the arms pass the face.

## Common mistakes
- Over-arching the lower back — common when standing; squeeze glutes and brace the core.
- Pressing in front of the body — increases shoulder strain; finish over the ears.
- Shrugging the shoulders to the ears — reduces deltoid work and irritates the neck.
- Using leg drive when strict pressing — save push press for intentional variations.

## Safety notes
- Reduce range or use a neutral grip if you feel pinching at the top of the press.
- Stop for sharp shoulder, neck, or upper-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if overhead pressing aggravates prior shoulder impingement.
