# Hack Squat

## Setup
- Position shoulders and back against the hack squat pads; feet shoulder-width on the platform.
- Place feet slightly forward on the platform for more knee emphasis, or back for more hip emphasis.
- Release the safety handles with knees slightly bent; brace the core before descending.
- Keep the lower back in contact with the pad throughout the set.

## Execution
- Lower by bending knees and hips together until thighs reach at least parallel or a controlled depth.
- Keep heels planted; knees track over the second and third toes.
- Drive through the mid-foot to extend knees and hips without locking aggressively.
- Maintain head and upper-back contact with the pad; do not lift the hips off the back support.

## Common mistakes
- Heels rising off the platform — reduces force production and stresses the knees.
- Knees collapsing inward — cue outward pressure or adjust foot width.
- Descending too fast into the bottom — bounces off soft tissue instead of muscular control.
- Locking the knees harshly at the top — unnecessary joint stress.

## Safety notes
- Adjust foot placement if you feel excessive knee or hip discomfort at depth.
- Stop for sharp knee, hip, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if machine squatting aggravates prior joint injuries.
