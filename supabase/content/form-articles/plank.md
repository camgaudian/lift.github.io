# Plank

## Setup
- Start on forearms and toes; elbows directly under shoulders, forearms parallel.
- Extend legs hip-width; squeeze glutes and brace the core as if bracing for a punch.
- Keep the head neutral; eyes look at the floor slightly ahead of the hands.

## Execution
- Hold the body in a straight line from head to heels; avoid sagging or piking.
- Breathe steadily through the nose or mouth without losing abdominal tension.
- Maintain shoulder blades slightly protracted; do not shrug up toward the ears.
- Hold for the prescribed time, then lower with control.

## Common mistakes
- Hips sagging toward the floor — shorten the hold or raise slightly onto knees.
- Piking hips too high — body should stay level, not form an inverted V.
- Holding breath — breathe while keeping the core engaged.
- Shrugging shoulders — press the floor away and keep the neck long.

## Safety notes
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back, shoulder, or wrist pain.
- Modify on knees or with forearms elevated if full plank is too demanding.
- Consult a qualified clinician for persistent discomfort or prior spinal issues.
