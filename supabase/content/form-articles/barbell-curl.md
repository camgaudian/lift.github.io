# Barbell Curl

## Setup
- Stand with feet hip-width apart, knees slightly soft.
- Grip the bar with an underhand, shoulder-width grip; elbows stay at your sides.
- Brace your core; keep chest up and shoulders pulled slightly back.
- Start with arms fully extended without locking the elbows aggressively.

## Execution
- Curl the bar toward your shoulders by flexing at the elbows only.
- Keep upper arms fixed — no swinging the bar with hip drive unless intentionally cheating on final reps.
- Squeeze at the top without letting shoulders roll forward.
- Lower under control to full extension for each rep.

## Common mistakes
- Excessive body English — reduces biceps tension and stresses the lower back.
- Elbows drifting forward or flaring wide — shifts load to the front delts.
- Partial range of motion — short reps limit growth stimulus.
- Wrists bending backward under load — keep wrists neutral or slightly flexed.

## Safety notes
- Use a weight that allows strict form for most reps.
- Stop if you feel sharp elbow or wrist pain.
- Consult a coach if elbow discomfort persists despite adjusting grip width and load.
