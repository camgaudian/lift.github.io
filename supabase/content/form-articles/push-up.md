# Push-Up

## Setup
- Start in a high plank; hands slightly wider than shoulder width, fingers spread.
- Body forms a straight line from head to heels; core and glutes engaged.
- Shoulder blades slightly protracted; head neutral, eyes toward the floor.

## Execution
- Lower the chest toward the floor by bending the elbows at roughly 45° from the torso.
- Descend until the chest is near the floor or upper arms are parallel to the ground.
- Press the floor away to return to the start; fully extend the elbows without locking aggressively.
- Maintain a rigid plank throughout; hips do not sag or pike.

## Common mistakes
- Hips sagging — shorten range or elevate the hands until core strength improves.
- Elbows flared to 90° — tuck elbows slightly to reduce shoulder stress.
- Partial range — use a full controlled descent for effective training.
- Head dropping or craning — keep the neck in line with the spine.

## Safety notes
- Scale to incline push-ups or knee push-ups if full reps are not yet manageable.
- Stop for sharp shoulder, wrist, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician for prior shoulder or wrist injuries.
