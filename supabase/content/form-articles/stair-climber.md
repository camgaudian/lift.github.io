# Stair Climber

## Setup
- Step onto the pedals with feet hip-width; hold handrails lightly for balance only.
- Select a moderate resistance and pace for your fitness level.
- Stand tall with a slight forward lean from the ankles, not the waist.
- Start with a slow cadence until the rhythm feels natural.

## Execution
- Drive through the full foot on each step; avoid staying on the balls of the feet only.
- Keep knees tracking in line with toes; do not let them collapse inward.
- Maintain steady breathing; increase pace or resistance gradually rather than all at once.
- Stay upright through the torso; minimize leaning heavily on the rails.

## Common mistakes
- Gripping the rails and pulling body weight up — reduces leg work and calorie burn.
- Shallow, fast steps with no full extension — limits glute and quad engagement.
- Excessive forward hunch — strains the lower back and reduces hip drive.
- Starting too hard — leads to early fatigue and poor form.

## Safety notes
- Stop if you feel dizziness, chest pain, or sharp knee or hip pain.
- Use the emergency stop if you lose balance or feel unwell.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician before high-intensity cardio if you have heart or joint conditions.
