# Walking

## Setup
- Wear supportive, comfortable shoes suited to the terrain and distance.
- Stand tall with shoulders relaxed; arms can swing naturally at the sides.
- Start at an easy pace for 3–5 minutes before increasing speed if desired.
- Plan a safe route with even footing; stay aware of traffic and obstacles.

## Execution
- Take natural strides; land softly on the heel and roll through to the toe.
- Keep the head up and eyes forward; avoid looking down at the ground continuously.
- Breathe steadily; increase pace or incline gradually rather than abruptly.
- Maintain an upright posture; slight forward lean only when walking uphill.

## Common mistakes
- Overstriding with a heavy heel strike — increases impact on knees and hips.
- Slouching or looking at a phone — strains the neck and reduces breathing efficiency.
- Starting too fast without a warm-up — can cause shin or calf discomfort.
- Wearing unsupportive footwear — increases foot and ankle fatigue.

## Safety notes
- Stay hydrated on longer walks; use sun protection and visibility gear as needed.
- Stop if you feel chest pain, dizziness, or sharp joint pain.
- Consult a qualified clinician before starting a walking program if you have cardiovascular or mobility limitations.
