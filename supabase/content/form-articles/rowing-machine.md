# Rowing Machine

## Setup
- Adjust the foot straps so the ball of the foot is secure; damper or resistance set appropriately.
- Sit tall on the seat; grip the handle with arms extended and shoulders relaxed.
- Hinge slightly forward from the hips at the catch; shins vertical, arms straight.

## Execution
- Drive through the legs first; then lean back slightly and pull the handle to the lower chest.
- Finish with legs extended, torso leaning back slightly, and elbows past the torso.
- Return in reverse order: arms extend, torso hinges forward, then knees bend toward the catch.
- Maintain a smooth stroke ratio; avoid rushing the recovery phase.

## Common mistakes
- Pulling with the arms before the legs drive — leg drive should initiate every stroke.
- Rounding the lower back at the catch — sit tall and brace the core.
- Hunching shoulders up — keep shoulders down and lats engaged on the pull.
- Short, choppy strokes — use full leg drive and a controlled recovery.

## Safety notes
- Build volume gradually; rowing is demanding on the lower back and hips.
- Stop for sharp lower-back, rib, or shoulder pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if you have prior lower-back or rib injuries.
