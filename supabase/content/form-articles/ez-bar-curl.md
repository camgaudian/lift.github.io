# EZ Bar Curl

## Setup
- Stand tall with feet hip-width; grip the EZ bar on the angled inner handles, palms up.
- Pin the elbows close to the torso; brace the core to limit sway.
- Start with arms fully extended; wrists follow the bar's natural angle.
- Keep shoulders down and chest lifted.

## Execution
- Curl the bar up by flexing the elbows; upper arms stay fixed at your sides.
- Squeeze the biceps at the top without letting the shoulders roll forward.
- Lower with control to full extension; maintain the EZ bar angle in the wrists.
- Exhale on the way up; avoid using hip thrust to start the rep.

## Common mistakes
- Swinging the torso — reduces biceps tension and stresses the lower back.
- Elbows drifting forward — shortens range and shifts load off the biceps.
- Gripping too wide or too narrow on the bends — awkward wrist angles; adjust hand placement.
- Rushing the negative — sacrifices control and time under tension.

## Safety notes
- The angled grip can ease wrist discomfort for some lifters but is not a fix for all elbow issues.
- Stop for sharp elbow, wrist, or forearm pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if curling consistently aggravates tendinopathy symptoms.
