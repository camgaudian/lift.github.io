# Cable Pullover

## Setup
- Attach a straight bar or rope to a high pulley.
- Face the stack; grip the attachment and step back with arms extended at shoulder height.
- Hinge slightly at the hips with a braced core; soft bend in elbows.
- Set feet hip-width apart for a stable base.

## Execution
- Pull the attachment down and forward in an arc toward your thighs or hips.
- Keep arms relatively straight; focus on lats and serratus driving the motion.
- Squeeze lats at the bottom without rounding the upper back excessively.
- Return under control until you feel a stretch through the lats and chest.

## Common mistakes
- Bending elbows too much — turns the pullover into a triceps pushdown.
- Overarching the lower back to reach the bottom position.
- Using momentum from a violent hip drive on each rep.
- Shrugging shoulders up instead of depressing them at the start.

## Safety notes
- Use a weight that allows a smooth arc without shoulder pinching at the top stretch.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder or lower-back pain.
- See a coach if overhead shoulder mobility limits your range consistently.
