# Incline Cable Fly

## Setup
- Set an incline bench between two low cable pulleys; attach single handles.
- Lie back with shoulder blades retracted; grab the handles with a slight bend in the elbows.
- Start with arms wide and roughly level with the upper chest, palms facing each other or slightly forward.
- Brace the core and keep feet flat on the floor.

## Execution
- Sweep the handles together in a wide arc, squeezing the chest at the top.
- Maintain a fixed elbow angle throughout; movement comes from the shoulder joint.
- Lower with control until you feel a stretch across the upper chest.
- Repeat without letting the shoulders roll forward at the bottom.

## Common mistakes
- Bending and straightening the elbows — turns the fly into a press.
- Using too much weight — causes jerking and shoulder strain.
- Overstretching at the bottom — puts the shoulder in a vulnerable position.
- Rushing the negative — reduces tension and control.

## Safety notes
- Start light to learn the arc and cable tension.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder or pec pain.
- Adjust bench angle or cable height if the stretch feels pinchy in the front of the shoulder.
