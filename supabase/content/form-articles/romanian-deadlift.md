# Romanian Deadlift

## Setup
- Stand with feet hip-width; bar at hip height or lift from the floor with a conventional start.
- Soft knee bend — not a squat; hips set back.
- Grip shoulder-width; shoulders packed; spine neutral.

## Execution
- Push hips back; lower the bar along the thighs with minimal knee bend.
- Stop when hamstrings limit depth or the back begins to round.
- Drive hips forward to return to standing; squeeze glutes at the top.
- Keep the bar close to the legs throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Bending knees too much — turns the movement into a conventional deadlift.
- Rounding the lower back — reduce range or weight.
- Bar drifting forward — increases spinal load.
- Hyperextending at lockout — finish tall without leaning back excessively.

## Safety notes
- Start light to learn the hip hinge pattern.
- Stop for sharp hamstring, hip, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if you have a history of back or hamstring injuries.
