# Tricep Pushdown

## Setup
- Attach a straight bar, rope, or V-bar to a high cable pulley.
- Stand facing the stack with feet shoulder-width; slight knee bend for stability.
- Grip the attachment; tuck elbows close to the sides at roughly 90°.
- Brace the core; keep the chest up and shoulders down.

## Execution
- Extend the elbows to push the attachment down until arms are straight.
- Squeeze the triceps at the bottom; keep elbows pinned to the torso.
- Return with control to the starting position without letting elbows drift forward.
- Exhale on the push; maintain a stable torso without leaning over the bar.

## Common mistakes
- Elbows flaring away from the body — reduces triceps isolation.
- Using body lean and momentum — shifts work to the shoulders and chest.
- Partial range of motion — missing full extension or stretch at the top.
- Shrugging shoulders — overuses the traps instead of the triceps.

## Safety notes
- Start with a manageable weight to protect the elbow tendons.
- Stop if you feel sharp elbow, wrist, or shoulder pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if you have prior elbow tendinopathy.
