# Overhead Tricep Extension

## Setup
- Stand or sit with feet shoulder-width; hold a dumbbell, EZ bar, or cable attachment with both hands.
- Raise the weight overhead with arms fully extended; elbows close to the head, pointing forward.
- Brace the core; keep the rib cage down and avoid excessive lower-back arch.

## Execution
- Lower the weight behind the head by bending only at the elbows.
- Keep upper arms stationary; forearms move through a controlled arc.
- Extend the elbows to return to the starting position; squeeze the triceps at lockout.
- Exhale on the way up; inhale on the way down.

## Common mistakes
- Flaring elbows outward — reduces triceps isolation and stresses the shoulders.
- Arching the lower back — brace the core and reduce weight if needed.
- Using momentum or swinging — keep the movement slow and controlled.
- Partial range — aim for a full stretch at the bottom without pain.

## Safety notes
- Start light until shoulder and elbow mobility feel comfortable overhead.
- Stop for sharp elbow, shoulder, or neck pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if you have prior shoulder or elbow injuries.
