# Box Jump

## Setup
- Choose a stable box height you can land on with full foot contact and controlled knees.
- Stand arm's length from the box; feet hip- to shoulder-width apart.
- Swing arms back, hinge slightly, and set your gaze on the landing surface.
- Clear the area — no obstacles around the box.

## Execution
- Dip into a quarter squat and swing arms forward to generate upward momentum.
- Jump onto the box, landing softly with hips and knees bent to absorb impact.
- Stand fully on the box before stepping down — do not rebound jump down unless trained for it.
- Step down under control and reset between reps.

## Common mistakes
- Box too high — leads to scraped shins, poor landings, or missed reps.
- Landing with stiff, straight legs — increases joint stress.
- Jumping down repeatedly from the box — high impact without advanced plyometric training.
- Starting too far from the box — causes horizontal drift and unstable landings.

## Safety notes
- Progress box height only when every rep lands quietly and in balance.
- Stop if you feel knee, ankle, or Achilles pain.
- Work with a coach on plyometric progressions if you are new to jumping or returning from injury.
