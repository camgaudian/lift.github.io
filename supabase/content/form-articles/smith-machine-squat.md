# Smith Machine Squat

## Setup
- Set the bar at upper-chest height; step under and position it across the upper traps or front shoulders.
- Feet slightly in front of or under the bar path, shoulder-width or slightly wider.
- Unrack by rotating the hooks; brace the core, chest up, eyes forward.

## Execution
- Sit back and down; knees track over the toes without collapsing inward.
- Descend until thighs are at least parallel or to comfortable depth with a neutral spine.
- Drive through mid-foot and heel to stand; lock out hips and knees at the top.
- Re-rack by rotating the bar onto the hooks at the end of the set.

## Common mistakes
- Feet too far forward or back — adjust so knees track comfortably over the toes at depth.
- Knees caving inward — actively push knees out in line with the toes.
- Heels lifting off the floor — reduce depth or adjust foot position.
- Partial depth — use a range you can control with a neutral spine.

## Safety notes
- Use the safety stops set just below your lowest comfortable depth.
- Stop for sharp knee, hip, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach before heavy Smith squats with prior knee or back issues.
