# Cable Crunch

## Setup
- Attach a rope to a high pulley; kneel facing the stack with the rope beside your head.
- Hold rope ends at your temples or ears — do not pull on your neck.
- Hips stay fixed over knees; set a slight posterior pelvic tilt to start.
- Choose a weight that allows a full crunch without using arm pull to move the stack.

## Execution
- Crunch down by flexing your spine and bringing ribs toward pelvis.
- Exhale through the hardest part of the rep; squeeze abs at the bottom.
- Return slowly to a stretched position without letting the weight yank you up.
- Keep hips stationary — the movement is spinal flexion, not hip hinge.

## Common mistakes
- Pulling with the arms instead of crunching with the abs.
- Sitting back onto the heels and turning it into a hip-dominant movement.
- Using excessive weight — shortens range and recruits hip flexors.
- Rushing the eccentric — reduces time under tension and control.

## Safety notes
- Keep neck neutral; hands guide the rope, they do not yank your head.
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back pain rather than abdominal fatigue.
- Consult a coach if kneeling bothers your knees — a standing cable crunch may be an alternative.
