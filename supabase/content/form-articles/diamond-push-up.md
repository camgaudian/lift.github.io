# Diamond Push-Up

## Setup
- Start in a high plank with hands together under the chest, thumbs and index fingers forming a diamond.
- Feet hip- to shoulder-width; body in a straight line from head to heels.
- Brace the core and squeeze the glutes to keep the hips from sagging.
- Position elbows close to the torso before descending.

## Execution
- Lower the chest toward the hands by bending the elbows back along the ribs.
- Descend until the chest nearly touches the hands or you reach a controlled depth.
- Press the floor away to return to the start; fully extend the elbows without locking harshly.
- Keep the head neutral and the shoulder blades moving smoothly through the rep.

## Common mistakes
- Elbows flaring wide — increases wrist and shoulder stress.
- Hips sagging or piking up — breaks tension and shifts load off the chest and triceps.
- Hands too far forward — turns the movement into an awkward incline press.
- Short range of motion — limits triceps and chest engagement.

## Safety notes
- Scale to knees or an incline if wrist pain appears in the narrow hand position.
- Stop for sharp wrist, elbow, or shoulder pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if narrow-grip pressing aggravates prior joint issues.
