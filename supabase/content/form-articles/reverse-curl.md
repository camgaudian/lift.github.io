# Reverse Curl

## Setup
- Stand with feet hip-width; hold a barbell or EZ bar with an overhand (pronated) grip.
- Arms hang at the sides; shoulders back, core braced, elbows close to the torso.
- Grip width roughly shoulder-width; wrists stay neutral.

## Execution
- Curl the bar up by flexing the elbows; keep the upper arms stationary.
- Lift until the forearms are roughly parallel to the floor or slightly higher.
- Lower with control to full extension; avoid swinging the torso.
- Exhale on the way up; squeeze the brachialis and forearms at the top.

## Common mistakes
- Using momentum or leaning back — reduce weight and keep the torso still.
- Elbows drifting forward — pin the upper arms to the sides throughout.
- Excessive wrist extension — maintain a straight, strong wrist position.
- Grip too wide or too narrow — adjust for comfort without stressing the wrists.

## Safety notes
- Start lighter than standard curls; forearms and elbows may fatigue quickly.
- Stop for sharp elbow, wrist, or forearm pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if you have prior elbow tendinopathy.
