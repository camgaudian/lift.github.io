# Standing Calf Raise

## Setup
- Position shoulders under the machine pads or hold dumbbells at your sides.
- Place the balls of the feet on the platform edge; heels hanging off.
- Stand tall with knees straight but not locked; feet hip-width or slightly narrower.
- Unrack or lift the load; brace the core for balance.

## Execution
- Rise onto the toes as high as possible; pause briefly at the top.
- Lower heels below the platform level for a full stretch at the bottom.
- Move through a controlled range; avoid bouncing at the bottom.
- Keep knees stable — do not bend them to cheat the rep.

## Common mistakes
- Partial range of motion — missing the stretch or full contraction.
- Using momentum and bouncing — reduces time under tension.
- Allowing knees to bend and straighten — shifts work away from the calves.
- Leaning forward excessively — reduces load on the calves.

## Safety notes
- Use a safety catch or support when using heavy machine loads.
- Stop if you feel sharp Achilles, ankle, or calf pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if you have a history of Achilles or plantar fascia issues.
