# Spider Curl

## Setup
- Set an incline bench to roughly 45–60°; lie chest-down with armpits at the top edge.
- Hold dumbbells or an EZ bar with an underhand grip; arms hang straight toward the floor.
- Keep the chest against the pad; feet flat on the floor for stability.

## Execution
- Curl the weight up by flexing the elbows; upper arms stay perpendicular to the floor.
- Squeeze the biceps at the top without lifting the elbows forward.
- Lower with control to full extension; avoid swinging the weights.
- Exhale on the way up; maintain contact with the bench throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Lifting the chest off the pad — reduce weight and stay pinned to the bench.
- Swinging the weights — use a strict, controlled tempo.
- Elbows drifting forward — keep upper arms fixed pointing toward the floor.
- Partial range — use a full stretch at the bottom without hyperextending the elbows.

## Safety notes
- Start lighter than standard curls; the stretched position loads the biceps significantly.
- Stop for sharp elbow or shoulder pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician for prior biceps tendon or elbow issues.
