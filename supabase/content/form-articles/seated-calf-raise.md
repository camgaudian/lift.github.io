# Seated Calf Raise

## Setup
- Sit on the calf raise machine or a bench with a block under the balls of the feet.
- Place the balls of the feet on the edge of the platform; heels hang off.
- Pad or bar rests on the lower thighs; knees bent at roughly 90°.

## Execution
- Lower the heels below the platform for a full stretch in the calves.
- Press through the balls of the feet to raise the heels as high as possible.
- Pause briefly at the top; squeeze the calves before lowering with control.
- Keep the movement strict; avoid bouncing at the bottom.

## Common mistakes
- Bouncing out of the bottom — use a controlled tempo through full range.
- Partial range — allow a full stretch and a high heel raise each rep.
- Using too much weight with shortened reps — prioritize range over load.
- Feet positioned incorrectly — balls of the feet on the edge, toes forward.

## Safety notes
- Start with moderate load; Achilles tendon responds best to progressive overload.
- Stop for sharp calf, ankle, or Achilles pain.
- Consult a qualified clinician if you have prior Achilles or ankle injuries.
