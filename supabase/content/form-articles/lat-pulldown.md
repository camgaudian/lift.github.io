# Lat Pulldown

## Setup
- Adjust the thigh pad so your legs are secured; grip the bar wider than shoulder width or as programmed.
- Sit tall with a slight lean back from the hips; chest up and core lightly braced.
- Start with arms extended overhead without letting the shoulders shrug up to the ears.
- Retract the shoulder blades slightly before initiating the pull.

## Execution
- Pull the bar down toward the upper chest by driving the elbows down and back.
- Squeeze the lats at the bottom; keep the torso stable without excessive swinging.
- Return the bar with control to full extension without letting the shoulders collapse forward.
- Exhale on the pull; maintain a neutral neck throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Pulling behind the neck — increases neck and shoulder injury risk for most lifters.
- Using body momentum — reduces lat engagement and stresses the lower back.
- Pulling the bar to the belly with excessive lean — shifts work away from the lats.
- Gripping too wide for your mobility — limits range and shoulder comfort.

## Safety notes
- Pull to the front of the chest unless a coach prescribes otherwise.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder, elbow, or neck pain.
- Use a weight you can control through the full range without jerking.
