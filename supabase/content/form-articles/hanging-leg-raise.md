# Hanging Leg Raise

## Setup
- Hang from a pull-up bar with an overhand grip slightly wider than shoulder width.
- Depress the shoulder blades slightly; avoid a full dead hang that shrugs the ears to the bar.
- Brace the core before lifting; legs together with toes pointed or flexed as preferred.
- Start from a still hang without swinging.

## Execution
- Raise the legs by flexing at the hips and curling the pelvis; lead with the knees or straight legs per variation.
- Lift until thighs pass parallel to the floor or higher if mobility and control allow.
- Lower with control to the start without swinging into the next rep.
- Exhale on the way up; keep the upper body stable.

## Common mistakes
- Swinging and using momentum — reduces ab work and stresses the shoulders.
- Shrugging the shoulders up to the ears — fatigues the grip and irritates the neck.
- Arching the lower back at the top — posterior tilt slightly to finish the rep.
- Dropping the legs uncontrolled — increases shoulder and spinal shear on the negative.

## Safety notes
- Build capacity with bent-knee raises or captain's chair variations before strict hanging work.
- Stop for sharp shoulder, grip, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if hanging leg raises aggravate shoulder or hip flexor issues.
