# Hip Adduction

## Setup
- On a seated adduction machine, sit with back against the pad and legs spread against the inner thigh pads.
- Adjust the seat so knees align with the machine axis; feet on the foot rests.
- Brace the core; hold the handles lightly without hunching forward.
- Start with legs apart and tension on the stack.

## Execution
- Squeeze the legs together by bringing the knees inward against the pads.
- Control the motion through the full range; pause briefly at the closed position.
- Return with control to the start without bouncing at the end range.
- Keep the pelvis stable on the seat throughout the set.

## Common mistakes
- Rocking the torso to force the legs together — reduces adductor engagement.
- Letting the weight stack crash between reps — increases joint stress and breaks tension.
- Gripping the handles excessively and rounding the upper back — poor posture reduces effectiveness.
- Using loads that require hip hiking or spinal twist to complete reps.

## Safety notes
- Adductor strains are common when loading too heavy too soon; progress gradually.
- Stop for sharp groin or inner-thigh pain, especially near the pubic attachment.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if adduction work aggravates groin or hip joint injuries.
