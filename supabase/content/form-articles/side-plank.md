# Side Plank

## Setup
- Lie on one side; stack the feet or stagger them for balance.
- Prop up on the forearm with the elbow directly under the shoulder.
- Lift the hips so the body forms a straight line from head to feet; free hand on hip or reaching up.

## Execution
- Hold the position with hips elevated; avoid sagging toward the floor.
- Brace the obliques and glutes; breathe steadily without losing tension.
- Keep the head in line with the spine; gaze forward.
- Hold for the prescribed time, then switch sides.

## Common mistakes
- Hips sagging — shorten the hold or bend the bottom knee for a modified side plank.
- Elbow too far from the shoulder — align the elbow under the shoulder for joint safety.
- Rotating the chest toward the floor — stack shoulders and hips vertically.
- Holding breath — maintain steady breathing throughout the hold.

## Safety notes
- Modify with knees bent if full side plank is too demanding.
- Stop for sharp shoulder, hip, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified clinician for prior shoulder or spinal issues.
