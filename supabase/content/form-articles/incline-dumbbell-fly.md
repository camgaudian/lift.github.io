# Incline Dumbbell Fly

## Setup
- Set the bench to 30–45°; lie back with dumbbells held above the upper chest, palms facing each other.
- Retract the shoulder blades; feet flat on the floor with a slight natural arch in the lower back.
- Start with a soft bend in the elbows that stays fixed throughout the set.
- Press the dumbbells up to the starting position if a spotter is not available.

## Execution
- Open the arms in a wide arc, lowering the dumbbells until you feel a stretch across the upper chest.
- Keep the elbow angle constant; do not press or pull with the triceps.
- Squeeze the chest to bring the dumbbells back together over the upper chest.
- Control the bottom position without letting the shoulders roll forward.

## Common mistakes
- Turning the movement into a press by bending the elbows excessively.
- Dropping the weights too deep — overstretches the shoulder capsule.
- Using momentum on the way up — reduces chest engagement.
- Letting the dumbbells drift toward the hips — changes the target angle.

## Safety notes
- Use manageable weights; dumbbell flies are not suited for maximal loads.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder or pec pain.
- Have a spotter help with the initial lift-off when using heavier dumbbells.
