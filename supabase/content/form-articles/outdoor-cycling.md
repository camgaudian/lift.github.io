# Outdoor Cycling

## Setup
- Adjust saddle height so the knee has a slight bend at the bottom of the pedal stroke.
- Set handlebar reach so you can ride with a neutral spine without overstretching.
- Check tire pressure, brakes, and gears before heading out; wear a helmet and visible gear as appropriate.
- Warm up with 5–10 minutes of easy spinning before harder efforts.

## Execution
- Pedal in smooth circles: push over the top, scrape through the bottom, pull through the back.
- Shift gears to maintain a sustainable cadence on climbs and flats.
- Stay relaxed through the shoulders and grip; keep the core lightly engaged for stability.
- Cool down with easy riding and hydrate throughout longer sessions.

## Common mistakes
- Saddle too low — overloads the knees and reduces power.
- Mashing a heavy gear with low cadence — increases knee stress on long rides.
- Locking the elbows and tensing the upper body — wastes energy and strains the neck.
- Skipping hydration on longer rides — reduces performance and recovery.

## Safety notes
- Follow local traffic laws and use lights in low visibility.
- Stop if you feel sharp knee, hip, or lower-back pain.
- Build distance and intensity gradually, especially on hilly terrain.
