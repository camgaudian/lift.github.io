# Treadmill Run

## Setup
- Start with the feet on the side rails; attach the safety clip if available.
- Select a comfortable walking pace before transitioning to a run.
- Stand centered on the belt; avoid running too close to the front or back.
- Wear supportive running shoes suited to your gait and foot type.

## Execution
- Land with a mid-foot strike under the hips; avoid overstriding with a heel-first landing far ahead.
- Keep a slight forward lean from the ankles; maintain relaxed shoulders and arms.
- Breathe rhythmically; increase speed or incline gradually rather than in large jumps.
- Look forward, not down at the console — keeps the neck and spine aligned.

## Common mistakes
- Holding the handrails while running — alters gait and reduces calorie burn.
- Overstriding — increases braking forces and knee stress.
- Jumping on or off a moving belt — major fall and injury risk.
- Starting at sprint pace without a warm-up — raises injury risk.

## Safety notes
- Always use the safety clip; know how to stop the belt quickly.
- Stop if you feel chest pain, dizziness, or sharp knee or shin pain.
- Consult a qualified clinician before intense running if you have cardiovascular or joint conditions.
