# Leg Curl

## Setup
- Adjust the leg curl machine so the pad rests on the lower calf or Achilles area, not the ankle joint.
- Lie face down (lying curl) or sit with hips and back against the pad (seated curl) as the machine requires.
- Grip the handles; align the knee joint with the machine pivot point.
- Start with legs extended and hips pressed firmly into the pad.

## Execution
- Curl the heels toward the glutes by flexing the knees; squeeze the hamstrings at the top.
- Keep the hips down and still throughout the rep.
- Lower with control to full extension without hyperextending the knees aggressively.
- Exhale on the curl; maintain steady tempo.

## Common mistakes
- Lifting the hips off the pad — reduces hamstring isolation and loads the lower back.
- Using momentum to swing the weight — often from too heavy a load.
- Setting the pad too high on the calf — stresses the ankle and reduces leverage.
- Partial reps at the top — missing the peak contraction.

## Safety notes
- Stop if you feel sharp knee or hamstring pain.
- Use a controlled weight; leg curls are not suited for maximal jerking loads.
- Consult a clinician if you have a recent hamstring or knee injury before loading heavily.
