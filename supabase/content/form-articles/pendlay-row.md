# Pendlay Row

## Setup
- Place a loaded barbell on the floor; stand with feet hip-width, bar over mid-foot.
- Hinge at the hips until the torso is roughly parallel to the floor; shins vertical.
- Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder width; back flat, chest up, core braced.

## Execution
- Pull the bar explosively to the lower chest or upper abdomen; elbows drive back.
- Lower the bar to a dead stop on the floor each rep; reset briefly between reps.
- Keep the torso angle fixed; avoid using leg drive or hip extension to lift the weight.
- Exhale on the pull; maintain a neutral neck and spine throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Bouncing the bar off the floor — each rep should start from a full stop.
- Rounding the lower back — reduce weight and set the hips back with a flat spine.
- Pulling with the arms only — initiate with the lats and mid-back, not the biceps.
- Standing up between reps — torso stays parallel for the entire set.

## Safety notes
- Use straps only when grip limits training, not to mask poor form.
- Stop for sharp lower-back or biceps pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if you have a history of lower-back issues.
