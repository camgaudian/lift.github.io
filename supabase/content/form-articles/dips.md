# Dips

## Setup
- Grip parallel bars with arms extended; shoulders down and slightly back.
- Start upright with a slight forward lean for chest emphasis, or more vertical for triceps focus.
- Cross ankles or keep legs straight below the hips; brace the core.
- Retract the shoulder blades before lowering into the first rep.

## Execution
- Lower by bending the elbows until upper arms are roughly parallel to the floor or slightly below.
- Keep the elbows tracking back, not flaring straight out to the sides.
- Press down through the hands to return to full extension without shrugging the shoulders.
- Control the descent; avoid dropping into the bottom position.

## Common mistakes
- Shrugging at the top — compresses the neck and reduces shoulder stability.
- Flaring elbows wide — increases shoulder impingement risk.
- Swinging or kicking the legs — uses momentum instead of upper-body strength.
- Going too deep without shoulder mobility — causes anterior shoulder pain.

## Safety notes
- Use an assisted dip machine or bands if you cannot control body weight through full range.
- Stop immediately for sharp front-of-shoulder or sternum pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if you have a history of shoulder instability or AC joint issues.
