# Swimming

## Setup
- Warm up with easy laps or drills before increasing pace or distance.
- Choose a stroke suited to your skill level — freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, or kick sets.
- Maintain a horizontal body line; head aligned with the spine, not lifted excessively.
- Use goggles and a comfortable breathing pattern for your stroke.

## Execution
- Rotate the body as a unit on freestyle and backstroke; avoid swimming flat.
- Reach forward on each stroke; pull through the water with a high elbow on freestyle.
- Exhale underwater on freestyle; inhale quickly when the mouth clears the surface.
- Kick from the hip with relaxed ankles; avoid excessive knee bend.

## Common mistakes
- Lifting the head too high to breathe — sinks the hips and increases drag.
- Over-kicking from the knees — wastes energy and disrupts body position.
- Holding breath underwater — leads to early fatigue and poor rhythm.
- Starting too fast without a warm-up — raises injury and cramp risk.

## Safety notes
- Swim in supervised areas; never swim alone in open water without proper safety measures.
- Stop if you feel chest pain, cramping, or dizziness.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician before intense swimming if you have shoulder or cardiovascular limitations.
