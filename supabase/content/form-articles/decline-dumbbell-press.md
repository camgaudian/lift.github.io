# Decline Dumbbell Press

## Setup
- Secure feet under the decline bench pads; hold a dumbbell in each hand at chest level.
- Set shoulder blades down and back; wrists neutral and stacked over elbows.
- Start with elbows slightly below the bench line and dumbbells at the lower chest.
- Use a spotter when handling heavy dumbbells on and off the bench.

## Execution
- Press the dumbbells up and slightly inward until arms are extended without locking aggressively.
- Lower with control until elbows reach roughly bench level or a comfortable stretch.
- Keep the dumbbells tracking over the lower chest throughout the rep.
- Exhale on the press; maintain steady foot and hip contact with the bench.

## Common mistakes
- Letting dumbbells drift toward the face — poor path and shoulder stress.
- Dropping the elbows too low behind the torso — increases shoulder strain.
- Uneven pressing — often from one arm dominating; match tempo and range on both sides.
- Using momentum by bouncing at the bottom — reduces muscle tension and control.

## Safety notes
- Control the descent; do not drop dumbbells at the end of a set without a safe plan.
- Stop if you feel pinching or sharp pain in the shoulders or chest.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician for persistent shoulder discomfort.
