# Dumbbell Fly

## Setup
- Lie on a flat bench with a dumbbell in each hand; feet flat on the floor.
- Press the dumbbells above the chest with a slight bend in the elbows; palms facing each other.
- Set shoulder blades down and back; maintain a stable rib cage and light lower-back arch.
- Keep wrists neutral and elbows at a fixed, soft angle throughout the set.

## Execution
- Open the arms in a wide arc, lowering the dumbbells out to the sides until you feel a chest stretch.
- Keep the elbow angle constant; do not press or curl the weight.
- Squeeze the chest to bring the dumbbells back together above the sternum.
- Control the descent; avoid dropping into the bottom stretch.

## Common mistakes
- Bending and straightening the elbows — turns the fly into a press and reduces pec isolation.
- Going too deep with heavy weight — overstretches the shoulders and pec tendons.
- Arching excessively off the bench — compromises shoulder stability.
- Clanking dumbbells at the top — indicates loss of tension and poor control.

## Safety notes
- Use moderate loads; flies are not suited to maximal heavy singles.
- Stop for sharp anterior shoulder or chest pain, especially at the bottom.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if fly variations consistently irritate the shoulders.
