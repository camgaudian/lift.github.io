# Glute Bridge

## Setup
- Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat, hip-width apart and close to the glutes.
- Arms at your sides, palms down; brace the core and tuck the pelvis slightly.
- Press the lower back gently toward the floor before lifting.
- Keep the chin slightly tucked and gaze toward the ceiling.

## Execution
- Drive through the heels and squeeze the glutes to lift the hips off the floor.
- Rise until the body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees at the top.
- Pause briefly without hyperextending the lower back; ribs stay down.
- Lower with control to the start without collapsing the pelvis.

## Common mistakes
- Pushing through the toes and lifting the heels — reduces glute emphasis.
- Over-arching at the top — lumbar extension instead of hip extension.
- Letting the knees cave inward — keep knees tracking over the second and third toes.
- Rushing reps — shortens time under tension and reduces glute activation.

## Safety notes
- Progress to single-leg bridges only when bilateral form is solid.
- Stop for sharp lower-back or hip pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if bridging aggravates existing back or hip conditions.
