# Pec Deck

## Setup
- Adjust the seat so handles or pads align with mid-chest height.
- Sit with back flat against the pad; feet flat on the floor.
- Grip the handles or place forearms on the pads; start with a slight stretch in the chest.

## Execution
- Bring the arms together in a smooth arc in front of the chest.
- Squeeze the chest at the end range without forcing the shoulders forward.
- Return with control to the starting position; maintain contact with the back pad.
- Keep a soft bend in the elbows throughout; do not lock out aggressively.

## Common mistakes
- Using too much weight and jerking the stack — reduces control and shoulder safety.
- Rolling shoulders forward at the squeeze — keep shoulder blades gently retracted.
- Locking the elbows straight — maintain a slight bend to protect the joints.
- Lifting off the seat — reduce weight and keep the torso stable against the pad.

## Safety notes
- Stop if you feel pinching or sharp pain in the front of the shoulder.
- Avoid forcing range if shoulder mobility is limited.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician for prior shoulder or pec injuries.
