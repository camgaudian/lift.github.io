# Rack Pull

## Setup
- Set the barbell on rack pins or blocks at mid-shin to just below the knee, depending on goal.
- Stand with feet hip-width, bar over mid-foot; grip just outside the legs.
- Hinge to the bar; flat back, chest up, lats engaged, core braced.

## Execution
- Drive through the floor; extend hips and knees together to stand tall.
- Keep the bar close to the body; lock out with hips fully extended and shoulders back.
- Lower the bar to the pins or blocks with control; reset grip and brace each rep if needed.
- Exhale on the way up; maintain a neutral spine throughout.

## Common mistakes
- Starting with a rounded back — set the hips and chest before pulling.
- Bar drifting forward — drag the bar up the legs; reduce weight if grip or position fails.
- Hyperextending the lower back at lockout — stand tall without leaning back excessively.
- Jerking the bar off the pins — build tension before each pull.

## Safety notes
- Use pins or blocks that allow a strong starting position without excessive rounding.
- Stop for sharp lower-back, hip, or biceps pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if you have a history of lower-back or disc issues.
