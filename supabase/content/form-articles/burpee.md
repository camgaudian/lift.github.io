# Burpee

## Setup
- Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, arms at your sides.
- Clear floor space — you need room to jump and lie flat.
- Brace your core before initiating the movement.
- Scale by stepping back instead of jumping if needed for your fitness level.

## Execution
- Squat down and place hands on the floor shoulder-width apart.
- Jump or step feet back to a plank; body forms a straight line from head to heels.
- Optional: perform a controlled push-up, then jump or step feet to your hands.
- Stand and jump vertically with hands overhead, or stand without the jump for a low-impact version.

## Common mistakes
- Sagging hips in the plank — stresses the lower back.
- Landing hard from the jump with stiff knees — increase impact on joints.
- Rushing reps with poor plank position — quality drops quickly under fatigue.
- Hands too narrow or too far forward — compromises shoulder stability.

## Safety notes
- Modify by stepping instead of jumping if you have knee, hip, or cardiovascular limitations.
- Stop if you feel chest pain, dizziness, or sharp wrist or shoulder pain.
- See a coach for scaled progressions if burpees are new or you are returning from injury.
