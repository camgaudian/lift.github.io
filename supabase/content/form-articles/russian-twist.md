# Russian Twist

## Setup
- Sit on the floor with knees bent; lean back slightly to engage the core, feet flat or lifted.
- Hold a weight plate, dumbbell, or medicine ball at chest height with both hands.
- Brace the core; keep the chest up and spine long.

## Execution
- Rotate the torso to one side, moving the weight toward the hip; hips stay facing forward.
- Rotate through the center to the opposite side in a controlled rhythm.
- Breathe steadily; avoid holding breath or collapsing the chest.
- Move from the thoracic spine; lower back stays stable.

## Common mistakes
- Rotating only the arms — turn through the torso, not just the hands.
- Rounding the lower back — reduce lean or foot elevation if form breaks down.
- Moving too fast with heavy weight — prioritize control over speed.
- Holding breath — exhale on each twist to maintain core engagement.

## Safety notes
- Start without weight or with feet down until core control is solid.
- Stop for sharp lower-back or hip pain.
- Consult a qualified clinician if you have prior disc issues or are early postpartum.
