# Hammer Curl

## Setup
- Stand tall with feet hip-width; hold dumbbells at your sides with palms facing each other (neutral grip).
- Pin the elbows close to the torso; brace the core to limit sway.
- Start with arms fully extended; shoulders down and chest lifted.
- Keep wrists neutral throughout the set.

## Execution
- Curl the dumbbells up by flexing the elbows; upper arms stay fixed.
- Squeeze the biceps and brachialis at the top without rolling the shoulders forward.
- Lower with control to full extension; maintain the neutral hand position.
- Alternate arms or curl both together based on programming; keep tempo consistent.

## Common mistakes
- Swinging the torso — reduces arm muscle work and stresses the lower back.
- Elbows drifting forward — shortens range and changes the leverage.
- Wrist bending sideways — keep wrists stacked with the forearms.
- Using weight that forces a hip thrust to start each rep.

## Safety notes
- Neutral grip can be friendlier on the wrists and elbows for some lifters.
- Stop for sharp elbow, wrist, or forearm pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if curling aggravates existing tendinopathy.
