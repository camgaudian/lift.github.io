# Wrist Curl

## Setup
- Sit on a bench with forearms resting on the thighs; wrists hanging just past the knees.
- Hold a dumbbell or barbell with an underhand grip; palms facing up.
- Keep the forearms flat on the thighs throughout the set.
- Start with wrists in a neutral or slightly extended position.

## Execution
- Curl the wrists upward as far as comfortable; squeeze the forearm flexors at the top.
- Lower with control to a full stretch at the bottom.
- Move through a controlled range; avoid bouncing the weight.
- Keep the forearms stationary — only the wrists move.

## Common mistakes
- Lifting the forearms off the thighs — reduces isolation of the wrist flexors.
- Using momentum and bouncing — reduces time under tension.
- Gripping too tightly — can cause unnecessary forearm fatigue before the set ends.
- Partial range of motion — missing the stretch or full contraction.

## Safety notes
- Use moderate weight; forearm tendons respond poorly to sudden load spikes.
- Stop if you feel sharp wrist, forearm, or elbow pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if you have carpal tunnel symptoms or prior wrist injury.
