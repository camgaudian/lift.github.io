# Stair Sprints

## Setup
- Choose a staircase with good traction, clear steps, and a handrail if needed for safety.
- Warm up with 5–10 minutes of easy climbing or walking before sprint intervals.
- Start at the bottom; plan a fixed number of steps or flights per sprint.
- Wear supportive shoes with grip; avoid loose footwear.

## Execution
- Sprint up the stairs driving through the full foot; pump the arms for momentum.
- Take quick, powerful steps; land softly on the mid-foot when possible.
- Walk or jog down slowly for recovery between sprints — do not sprint downhill.
- Keep the torso slightly forward from the ankles; eyes on the steps ahead.

## Common mistakes
- Skipping steps recklessly — increases fall risk on unfamiliar stairs.
- Sprinting downhill — high impact and injury risk to knees and ankles.
- Starting intervals without a warm-up — raises injury risk.
- Leaning too far forward from the hips — can cause loss of balance.

## Safety notes
- Ensure the stairwell is clear of obstacles and other people.
- Stop immediately if you feel sharp knee, ankle, or calf pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if you have cardiovascular or lower-limb limitations.
