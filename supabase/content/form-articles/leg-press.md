# Leg Press

## Setup
- Sit in the leg press with back and head flat against the pad; feet on the platform shoulder-width apart.
- Place feet so knees track over toes; lower on the platform emphasizes quads, higher emphasizes glutes and hamstrings.
- Release the safety handles with knees slightly bent; never lock out at the top with the safeties disengaged.
- Grip the side handles and brace the core before descending.

## Execution
- Lower the platform by bending the knees until roughly 90° or slightly deeper if mobility allows.
- Keep heels down and knees tracking in line with the feet throughout.
- Drive through mid-foot to extend the legs without locking the knees forcefully at the top.
- Exhale on the press; maintain continuous contact between the lower back and the seat.

## Common mistakes
- Lower back rounding off the pad at depth — often from going too deep or poor hip mobility.
- Knees caving inward — increases knee stress.
- Locking out hard at the top — hyperextends the knees under load.
- Hands on the knees pushing — reduces leg work and can compromise back position.

## Safety notes
- Do not let the lower back peel off the pad; reduce depth or adjust foot placement if it does.
- Stop if you feel sharp knee or lower-back pain.
- Always re-engage safeties before exiting the machine.
