# Goblet Squat

## Setup
- Hold a dumbbell or kettlebell at chest height, cupping one end with both hands close to the sternum.
- Stand with feet shoulder-width or slightly wider; toes turned out moderately.
- Brace the core; keep the elbows pointing down inside the knees at the bottom.
- Maintain an upright torso; gaze forward or slightly down.

## Execution
- Sit down between the hips by bending knees and hips together.
- Descend until thighs are at least parallel or as deep as mobility allows with a neutral spine.
- Drive through the mid-foot to stand; keep the weight close to the chest throughout.
- Exhale on the way up; knees track in line with the toes.

## Common mistakes
- Letting the weight drift away from the chest — causes forward lean and loss of balance.
- Heels lifting off the floor — often from ankle restriction or excessive forward lean.
- Knees collapsing inward — cue "spread the floor" or reduce depth temporarily.
- Rounding the upper back — reset bracing or use a lighter load.

## Safety notes
- The goblet position helps teach squat depth but is not a substitute for mobility work.
- Stop for sharp knee, hip, or lower-back pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if squatting consistently provokes joint discomfort.
