# Close-Grip Bench Press

## Setup
- Lie on a flat bench with eyes under the bar; feet flat on the floor.
- Grip the bar with hands roughly shoulder-width — narrower than a standard bench press.
- Retract and depress shoulder blades; maintain a slight arch if that is your normal bench setup.
- Unrack to locked-out arms over the shoulders; brace before descending.

## Execution
- Lower the bar to the lower chest or upper sternum with elbows tucked roughly 30–45° from the torso.
- Touch lightly without bouncing; keep wrists stacked over elbows.
- Press straight up or slightly back toward the rack, locking out without flaring elbows wide.
- Maintain leg drive and upper-back tightness throughout the set.

## Common mistakes
- Grip too narrow — excessive wrist and elbow stress without extra triceps benefit.
- Elbows flaring to 90° — increases shoulder strain.
- Losing upper-back tightness and letting shoulders roll forward off the bench.
- Bouncing the bar off the chest — reduces control and increases injury risk.

## Safety notes
- Use a spotter or safeties when training near failure.
- Stop if you feel sharp wrist, elbow, or shoulder pain.
- See a coach to find the grip width that balances triceps work with joint comfort.
