# Step-Up

## Setup
- Place a sturdy box or bench at knee height or slightly below; ensure it will not slide.
- Stand facing the box with feet hip-width; hold dumbbells at sides if using load.
- Brace the core; keep the chest up and eyes forward.
- Place the entire working foot flat on top of the box before driving up.

## Execution
- Drive through the heel and mid-foot of the lead leg to stand on the box.
- Bring the trailing foot up to meet the lead foot or step through to the floor.
- Keep the knee of the working leg tracking over the toes; minimize pushing off the back foot.
- Lower with control by stepping down or reversing the movement.

## Common mistakes
- Pushing off the back leg excessively — reduces load on the working leg.
- Collapsing knee inward on the step — often from weak glutes or poor foot placement.
- Using a box that is too high — causes hip shift and compensatory lean.
- Dropping down uncontrolled on the descent — increases knee stress.

## Safety notes
- Confirm the platform is stable and rated for your body weight plus load.
- Stop if you feel sharp knee, hip, or ankle pain.
- Consult a qualified coach if balance is a limiting factor before adding heavy load.
