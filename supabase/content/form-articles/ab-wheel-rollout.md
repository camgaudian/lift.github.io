# Ab Wheel Rollout

## Setup
- Kneel on a padded surface with the ab wheel in front of you, hands on the handles.
- Set the wheel directly under your shoulders; brace your core and squeeze your glutes lightly.
- Start with a short range — roll toward a wall or stop before your lower back can sag.
- Keep a neutral neck; look at the floor a few inches ahead of the wheel.

## Execution
- Roll forward slowly while maintaining a straight line from knees (or feet) through hips to shoulders.
- Extend only as far as you can control without your lower back arching or hips dropping.
- Pull back using your abs and lats; think of bringing your ribs toward your pelvis.
- Exhale on the return; reset your brace before the next rep.

## Common mistakes
- Letting the lower back collapse at full extension — reduces core tension and stresses the spine.
- Rolling too far too soon — quality beats distance.
- Using only hip flexors to pull back instead of engaging the entire anterior core.
- Shrugging shoulders toward ears — keep shoulder blades stable.

## Safety notes
- Progress from kneeling to standing only after you can control full kneeling rollouts without lumbar sag.
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back or shoulder pain.
- See a coach if you cannot keep your spine neutral even at a short range.
