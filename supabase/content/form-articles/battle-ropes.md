# Battle Ropes

## Setup
- Anchor the ropes securely; stand facing the anchor with feet shoulder-width apart.
- Hold one end in each hand; hinge slightly at the hips with a braced core.
- Choose rope thickness and length appropriate for your fitness level.
- Start with shorter intervals and build duration as conditioning improves.

## Execution
- Create waves by alternating arm strokes or moving both arms together, depending on the drill.
- Generate power from the hips and core, not just the arms — stay athletic and balanced.
- Maintain steady breathing; match wave size to your target intensity.
- Keep shoulders down and away from ears throughout the set.

## Common mistakes
- Standing upright and arm-only whipping — limits power and fatigues shoulders quickly.
- Holding breath during hard intervals — reduces performance and increases dizziness risk.
- Waves that travel inconsistently — often from uneven effort or poor anchor setup.
- Gripping too tightly for long sets — leads to forearm burnout before cardiovascular limit.

## Safety notes
- Clear space around you; ropes can snap outward unexpectedly.
- Stop if you feel lightheaded, chest pain, or sharp shoulder discomfort.
- See a coach for progression if high-intensity rope work is new to you.
