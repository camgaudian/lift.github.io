# Superman

## Setup
- Lie face down on a mat with arms extended overhead and legs straight.
- Keep the neck neutral — look at the floor, not up.
- Engage the core lightly before lifting; breathe steadily.
- Position feet hip-width for a stable base.

## Execution
- Simultaneously lift the arms, chest, and legs off the floor.
- Hold briefly at the top; squeeze the glutes and upper back.
- Lower with control back to the starting position.
- Move through a range you can control without arching the neck.

## Common mistakes
- Hyperextending the neck to look forward — strains the cervical spine.
- Jerking into the lift — use a slow, controlled raise and lower.
- Lifting too high and compressing the lower back — focus on length, not height.
- Holding breath throughout — breathe steadily during reps.

## Safety notes
- Skip or modify if you have acute lower-back pain or recent spinal injury.
- Stop if you feel sharp back, hip, or shoulder pain.
- Consult a qualified clinician for persistent discomfort or prior back conditions.
