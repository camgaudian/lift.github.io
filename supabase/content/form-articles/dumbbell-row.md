# Dumbbell Row

## Setup
- Place one knee and the same-side hand on a bench; opposite foot on the floor for balance.
- Hold a dumbbell in the free hand with arm extended; back flat and parallel to the floor.
- Brace the core; let the shoulder blade protract slightly at the bottom.
- Keep the head in line with the spine; gaze toward the floor.

## Execution
- Row the dumbbell toward the hip by driving the elbow back, not out wide.
- Squeeze the shoulder blade toward the spine at the top without shrugging the neck.
- Lower with control until the arm is extended and the lat stretches.
- Keep the torso still; avoid rotating the hips to heave the weight.

## Common mistakes
- Jerking the torso for momentum — reduces back engagement and strains the spine.
- Rowing too high toward the shoulder — shifts emphasis away from the lats.
- Rounding the lower back — increases injury risk; reset posture or reduce load.
- Short range of motion — failing to fully extend at the bottom limits development.

## Safety notes
- Keep the spine neutral; support the lower back with bracing, not excessive rounding.
- Stop for sharp lower-back or shoulder pain.
- Consult a qualified coach or clinician if rowing aggravates prior back or shoulder issues.
