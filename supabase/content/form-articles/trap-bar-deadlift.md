# Trap Bar Deadlift

## Setup
- Step inside the trap bar; feet hip-width with shins near the handles.
- Hinge at the hips and grip the handles; chest up and shoulders over or slightly in front of the handles.
- Brace the core; pull slack from the bar before lifting.
- Align the bar over mid-foot; look forward or slightly down to keep a neutral neck.

## Execution
- Drive through the floor; extend knees and hips together.
- Keep the chest up and back flat as the bar rises.
- Lock out at the top with hips and knees fully extended; stand tall without leaning back.
- Lower by pushing hips back first; control the bar to the floor or platform.

## Common mistakes
- Hips rising before the bar moves — shifts load to the lower back.
- Rounding the upper back at setup — increases injury risk under load.
- Jerking the bar off the floor — breaks tension and form.
- Hyperextending at lockout — unnecessary lumbar stress.

## Safety notes
- Use bumper plates and a flat platform when resetting reps from height.
- Stop if you feel sharp lower-back, hip, or knee pain.
- Consult a qualified coach for form checks before adding significant load.
