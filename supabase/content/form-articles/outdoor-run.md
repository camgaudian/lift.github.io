# Outdoor Run

## Setup
- Choose appropriate footwear for your surface and distance; tie laces securely.
- Start with 3–5 minutes of easy walking or jogging to warm up the legs and cardiovascular system.
- Stand tall with a slight forward lean from the ankles, not the hips.
- Relax the shoulders, bend the elbows roughly 90°, and keep the gaze forward.

## Execution
- Land with feet under your hips on mid-foot or forefoot depending on pace and terrain.
- Drive the knees forward with a quick, light cadence; avoid overstriding with a heavy heel strike.
- Swing the arms forward and back, not across the body.
- Breathe rhythmically; ease pace on hills and accelerate gradually on descents.

## Common mistakes
- Overstriding — increases braking force and impact on the knees and shins.
- Heel striking far in front of the body — common with overstriding and slow cadence.
- Tension in the shoulders and jaw — wastes energy and can cause neck discomfort.
- Increasing mileage or speed too quickly — raises overuse injury risk.

## Safety notes
- Run facing traffic on roads without sidewalks and stay aware of your surroundings.
- Stop if you feel sharp joint pain, dizziness, or chest discomfort.
- Build weekly volume by no more than roughly 10% when increasing training load.
