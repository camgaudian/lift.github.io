# Incline Dumbbell Press

## Setup
- Set the bench to 30–45°; sit with dumbbells on your thighs and lie back in one smooth motion.
- Position the dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing forward or slightly angled.
- Retract and depress the shoulder blades; feet flat on the floor.
- Wrists stacked over elbows at the bottom of the range.

## Execution
- Press the dumbbells up and slightly together over the upper chest without clanking them.
- Lower with control until the upper arms are roughly parallel to the floor or slightly below.
- Keep elbows at roughly 45–60° from the torso throughout the rep.
- Exhale on the press; maintain contact between the upper back and the bench.

## Common mistakes
- Setting the incline too steep — overemphasizes the front delts.
- Flaring elbows to 90° — increases shoulder stress.
- Arching the lower back excessively — reduces stability.
- Pressing the dumbbells too far forward or backward — creates an awkward shoulder angle.

## Safety notes
- Kick the dumbbells up carefully or use a spotter for heavy sets.
- Stop if you feel sharp shoulder or chest pain.
- Consult a qualified coach for form checks before pushing heavy loads.
